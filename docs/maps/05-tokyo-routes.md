## Day 1 | Sep 9 -- Shinjuku Arrival

### Route: Hotel Check-in → Shinjuku Exploration

**Distance:** ~3 km walking | **Time:** 3-4 hours

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Arrival** | Tokyo Station (from Shinkansen) | - |
| **Transfer to Shinjuku** | JR Yamanote Line (15 min) | 20 min |
| **Check-in** | Hotel in Shinjuku area | - |
| **Shinjuku neighborhood** | Explore surroundings | 60 min |
| **Shinjuku Gyoen** | Optional: National garden (Y500) | 45-60 min |
| **Evening: Golden Gai** | Tiny bar hopping | 60-90 min |
| **Late dinner: Ramen at Fuunji** | Tsukemen (dipping noodles) | 30-45 min |

**Walking route details:**

1. **From Tokyo Station:** Take JR Yamanote Line to **Shinjuku Station** (15 min). Use the **east exit** for Golden Gai access or **south exit** for Fuunji.

2. **Shinjuku Gyoen (Optional):** Beautiful national garden with three different garden styles. Great for a peaceful walk after the Shinkansen ride.

3. **Golden Gai (Evening):** Labyrinth of ~200 tiny bars in six narrow alleys. Most seat 6-10 people. Some have cover charges (Y300-1,000). Look for bars with English signs or "Tourists Welcome."

4. **Fuunji (Late Night):** Located south of Shinjuku Station. Famous for tsukemen (thick noodles dipped in rich tonkotsu-gyokai broth). Expect a queue snaking across the street -- worth the wait. Order via vending machine at entrance.

## Day 2 | Sep 10 -- Asakusa + Akihabara

### Route: Historic Temple → Electric Town

**Distance:** ~4 km walking | **Time:** 5-6 hours

**Morning: Asakusa / Senso-ji**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Metro to Asakusa** | Ginza Line from Shinjuku (via transfer) | 25 min |
| **Senso-ji Temple** | Tokyo's oldest temple (founded 628 AD) | 45-60 min |
| **Kaminarimon Gate** | Iconic red lantern gate | 15 min |
| **Nakamise-dori** | Shopping street to temple | 30-45 min |

**Pro tip:** Visit Senso-ji **early morning (before 9 AM)** for fewer crowds. The temple is beautifully lit in the evening, another great option.

**Afternoon: Akihabara**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Transfer to Akihabara** | Metro or JR from Asakusa | 15 min |
| **Electric Town** | Anime, manga, retro gaming, electronics | 2-3 hours |
| **Super Potato** | Legendary retro gaming store | 30-45 min |
| **Radio Kaikan** | 30+ specialty shops (figures, cards, models) | 30-45 min |
| **Animate** | Japan's largest anime goods chain | 30 min |
| **Maid cafe lunch** | @Home Cafe (optional) | 45-60 min |
| **Explore back streets** | Hidden specialty shops | 60 min |

**Pro tip:** Akihabara's back streets off Chuo-dori have hidden gems and smaller specialty shops. Budget 3-5 hours for a thorough visit.

**Evening: Yurakucho Yakitori**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **JR to Yurakucho** | From Akihabara | 10 min |
| **Yakitori under tracks** | Izakayas between Yurakucho & Shimbashi | 60-90 min |

**Yurakucho:** The stretch of izakayas and yakitori joints under the JR train tracks is a quintessential Tokyo experience. Smoke, grilled chicken skewers, cold beer, salarymen unwinding. Peak atmosphere is weekday evenings (6-9 PM). No reservations needed -- find an open stool. Budget ~Y2,000-3,000 for skewers + beer.

## Day 3 | Sep 11 -- Hakone Day Trip

### Route: The Classic Loop Course

**Distance:** Minimal walking (mostly transport) | **Time:** 10-12 hours

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **7:30 AM Departure** | Shinjuku Station (Odakyu Line) | - |
| **Romancecar to Hakone-Yumoto** | 85 min, scenic reserved seats | 90 min |
| **Tozan Railway to Gora** | 40 min, switchback mountain train | 45 min |
| **Cable car to Sounzan** | 10 min, steep ascent | 15 min |
| **Ropeway to Owakudani** | 30 min, volcanic valley | 30 min |
| **Black eggs at Owakudani** | Said to add 7 years to life (Y500 for 5) | 15 min |
| **Ropeway to Togendai** | Continue over volcanic valley | 30 min |
| **Pirate ship on Lake Ashi** | To Moto-Hakone, Mt. Fuji views (weather) | 30 min |
| **Lunch at Moto-Hakone** | Lakeside restaurants | 45-60 min |
| **Bus to Hakone-Yumoto** | 35 min | 40 min |
| **Optional: Open Air Museum** | Gora area (Y1,600) | 90 min |
| **Romancecar to Shinjuku** | 85 min return | 90 min |

**Total cost:** Hakone Free Pass (Y6,100) + Romancecar round trip (Y2,400) = **~Y8,500 ($57 USD)**

**Pro tips:**
- **Book Romancecar in advance** at Odakyu's website or Shinjuku Station
- **Mt. Fuji visibility in September:** Low to moderate (~30-40%). Cloud cover builds throughout the day. Best chance: early morning before 9 AM. Don't plan trip solely for Fuji views -- enjoy the loop course itself.
- **Weather warning:** Rain or high winds will close ropeway. Have backup Tokyo plan ready.

## Day 4 | Sep 12 -- Fuji-Q Highland

### Route: Shinjuku → Theme Park at Fuji Base

**Distance:** ~5 km walking in park | **Time:** 10-12 hours

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **7:00 AM Departure** | Shinjuku Expressway Bus Terminal | - |
| **Highway bus to Fuji-Q** | ~1h 40m (Y2,200 one-way) | 100 min |
| **Arrive Fuji-Q** | Before opening (8:30 AM Saturdays) | - |
| **Eejanaika** | 4D coaster, 14 inversions (Priority Pass) | 30 min |
| **Takabisha** | 121-degree drop (Priority Pass) | 30 min |
| **Fujiyama** | Classic coaster, Mt. Fuji views | 30 min |
| **Lunch** | Food Stadium or Fujiyama Burger Shop | 45 min |
| **Fujiyama Tower** | 55m observation, Mt. Fuji photos | 30 min |
| **Ferris wheel** | Shining Flower, unobstructed Fuji views | 15 min |
| **Re-ride favorites** | Or explore Naruto village area | 60-90 min |
| **5:30 PM Departure** | Bus back to Shinjuku | 100 min |
| **Dinner in Koenji** | Koenji Gogo (yakitori, tebasaki wings) | 60-90 min |

**Getting there options:**
| Option | Time | Cost (One-Way) | Notes |
|--------|------|----------------|-------|
| **Highway Bus** | ~1h 40m | Y2,200 ($15) | Cheapest; departs Shinjuku Bus Terminal |
| **Q-Pack (combo)** | ~1h 40m | Y10,000 ($67) RT bus + 1-Day Pass | Best value |
| **Fuji Excursion Train** | ~2h | Y4,130 ($28) | Most comfortable; direct from JR Shinjuku |

**Pro tips:**
- **Arrive before park opening** (8:30 AM on Saturdays). Lines form early.
- **Buy Priority Passes** immediately via Fuji-Q app for Eejanaika and Takabisha -- sell out on busy days (~Y1,000 per ride).
- **Ride order:** Takabisha or Eejanaika first (longest waits), then Fujiyama, then observation/Ferris wheel in afternoon.
- **Download Fuji-Q app** for real-time wait times.
- **Weather warning:** Rain or high winds will close coasters. Check forecast and have backup Tokyo plan.

**Koenji Gogo (Dinner):** From Shinjuku, JR Chuo Line 8 min to Koenji Station. Yakitori, tebasaki (chicken wings), chef-recommended skewers. Best on weekday evenings; explore standing bars after.

## Day 5 | Sep 13 -- Harajuku + Shibuya

### Route: Youth Culture → Iconic Crossing

**Distance:** ~3 km walking | **Time:** 5-6 hours

**Morning: Harajuku / Meiji Shrine**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **JR to Harajuku** | From Shinjuku (7 min on Yamanote) | 10 min |
| **Meiji Shrine** | Serene forested Shinto shrine | 30-45 min |
| **Takeshita Street** | Pedestrian street, trendy fashion | 45-60 min |
| **Lunch** | Crepes on Takeshita or Cat Street cafe | 30-45 min |
| **Cat Street** | Upscale/indie fashion | 30-45 min |

**Pro tip:** Meiji Shrine is best visited in the morning when it's peaceful. Takeshita Street is extremely crowded on weekends -- expect to shuffle.

**Afternoon: Shibuya**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Walk to Shibuya** | From Harajuku (15 min) or JR 2 min | 20 min |
| **Shibuya Crossing** | World's busiest pedestrian crossing | 15 min |
| **Shibuya Sky** | Observation deck (Y2,000) | 45-60 min |
| **Hachiko statue** | Quick photo op outside station | 5 min |
| **Shibuya shopping** | 109, Parco, Hikarie | 60-90 min |

**Shibuya Crossing views:**
- **Shibuya Sky** (paid): Best views, 2,000 yen
- **Starbucks at Tsutaya** (free with drink): 2nd floor of Shibuya Tsutaya
- **Mag's Park rooftop** (paid): Y500, less crowded

**Evening: teamLab Borderless**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Metro to Kamiyacho** | Hibiya Line from Shibuya | 15 min |
| **teamLab Borderless** | Immersive digital art museum | 120-150 min |
| **Dinner: Ebisu Yokocho** | 21 food stalls under the tracks | 60-90 min |

**teamLab Borderless:** **Must book online in advance** -- sells out. Allow 2-3 hours. Wear white clothing for better photo effects. Location: Azabudai Hills, 2-min walk from Kamiyacho Station.

**Ebisu Yokocho:** From Kamiyacho, JR to Ebisu (2 min). 21 food stalls -- yakitori, gyutan, oden, gyoza. Hop between stalls, buzzing till 2 AM. Budget ~Y1,800 per person.

## Day 6 | Sep 14 -- Tsukiji + Tokyo Tower

### Route: Fish Market → Depachika → City Views

**Distance:** ~4 km walking | **Time:** 6-7 hours

**Morning: Tsukiji Outer Market**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **7:00 AM Arrival** | Metro to Tsukiji or Kabuto-cho | - |
| **Tsukiji Outer Market** | Fresh sushi, street food breakfast | 90-120 min |
| **Sushizanmai** | Conveyor belt sushi or counter | 30-45 min |

**Pro tip:** Tsukiji Outer Market is best early morning (7-10 AM). Many stalls close by early afternoon. Go for: fresh sushi, tamagoyaki (sweet egg), grilled seafood on sticks, fresh uni, tuna donburi.

**Midday: Ginza + Lunch**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Walk to Ginza** | From Tsukiji (10 min) | 15 min |
| **Ginza window shopping** | Luxury brands, depachika basements | 45-60 min |
| **Lunch from depachika** | Department store bento | 30-45 min |

**Depachika tip:** Visit basement food floors of Mitsukoshi or Matsuya in Ginza. Beautifully packaged foods, bento boxes, wagyu. Go 30-60 min before closing for discounts.

**Afternoon: Tokyo Tower OR Skytree**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Option A: Tokyo Tower** | 333m, main deck Y1,200 | 45-60 min |
| **Option B: Tokyo Skytree** | 634m, main deck Y2,100 | 45-60 min |

**Recommendation:** Skytree for the views; Tokyo Tower for the vibes (especially at night). Both are excellent -- choose based on location preference.

**Late Afternoon: Ueno Park (Optional)**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **JR to Ueno** | From Ginza (10 min) | 15 min |
| **Ueno Park** | Free, museums, temples | 45-60 min |
| **Ameyoko market** | Bustling market street | 30-45 min |

**Evening: Soba + Shinjuku**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **JR from Ueno to Kanda** | 5 min | 10 min |
| **Dinner: Kanda Yabu Soba** | Handmade soba since 1880 | 45-60 min |
| **Return to Shinjuku** | JR Yamanote Line | 15 min |
| **Farewell drinks** | Golden Gai or Kabukicho | 60-90 min |

**Kanda Yabu Soba:** Try the duck soba or tempura seiro. Finish with soba-yu (noodle water) in your dipping broth -- traditional way to enjoy every bit of flavor.

## Weather Considerations (Mid-September)

| | Early Sep | Mid Sep |
|--|----------|---------|
| **High** | 29-31°C (84-88°F) | 26-28°C (79-82°F) |
| **Low** | 22-24°C (72-75°F) | 20-22°C (68-72°F) |
| **Rain** | ~19 rainy days in the month | Same |
| **Humidity** | 73-82% | Gradually decreasing |

**Still hot and humid.** Pack light, breathable clothing. Typhoon season -- 1-2 typhoons possible in September (usually brief, half-day to two days of heavy rain).

**Backup indoor activities:**
- **teamLab Borderless** -- book in advance
- **Shopping malls** -- Ginza, Shibuya, Shinjuku
- **Department stores** -- Isetan, Takashimaya, Mitsukoshi
- **Museums** -- Tokyo National Museum (Ueno), Mori Art Museum (Roppongi)
