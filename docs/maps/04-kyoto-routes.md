# Kyoto Walking Routes
## Day-by-Day Neighborhood Guides (Sep 5-9, 2026)

## Day 1 | Sep 5 -- Eastern Kyoto

### Route: Nishiki Market → Kiyomizu-dera → Gion

**Distance:** ~4 km walking | **Time:** 4-5 hours

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Arrival from Osaka** | JR Special Rapid to Kyoto Station | 30 min |
| **Check-in** | Hotel drop-off | - |
| **Nishiki Market** | "Kyoto's Kitchen," 100+ food stalls | 60-90 min |
| **Walk to Higashiyama** | ~20 min through downtown | 20 min |
| **Kiyomizu-dera** | Historic wooden temple, city views | 45-60 min |
| **Ninenzaka/Sannenzaka** | Traditional approach streets, souvenir shops | 30-45 min |
| **Gion district** | Geisha/maiko spotting (5-7 PM best) | 45-60 min |
| **Dinner** | Pontocho Alley side street (obanzai at Shutei Bankara) | 60-90 min |

**Walking route details:**

1. **Nishiki Market (Start here for lunch):** Take subway to **Shijo Station**. Nishiki Market is a short walk. This covered 5-block market has been Kyoto's kitchen for 400 years. Try: yuba (tofu skin), tsukemono (pickles), dashimaki tamago (rolled omelette).

2. **Walk to Higashiyama:** From Shijo, walk east toward **Yasaka Shrine** and continue uphill toward **Kiyomizu-dera**. The walk takes about 20 minutes through atmospheric streets.

3. **Kiyomizu-dera:** The famous wooden stage offers sweeping views. Best visited early morning (6 AM) or late afternoon when tour groups depart.

4. **Ninenzaka & Sannenzaka:** The preserved stone-paved streets leading to/from Kiyomizu-dera are lined with traditional shops selling snacks, crafts, and souvenirs.

5. **Gion at dusk:** Walk west from Kiyomizu-dera through preservation districts to **Hanamikoji Street** in Gion. Best time for geiko/maiko spotting is 5-7 PM. Be respectful -- no blocking paths, no grabbing for photos.

**Dinner in Pontocho:**
- **Shutei Bankara** -- Obanzai (Kyoto home-style cooking) + sake, hidden off Pontocho alley
- Reservations recommended; counter seats give cooking view

## Day 2 | Sep 6 -- Fushimi Inari + Arashiyama

### Route: Early Morning Torii Gates → Bamboo Grove → Golden Pavilion

**Distance:** ~5 km walking | **Time:** Full day

**Early Morning: Fushimi Inari (CRITICAL -- Go at 6 AM!)**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **6:00 AM Departure** | Early start to beat crowds | - |
| **JR to Inari Station** | JR Nara Line from Kyoto (2 min) | 5 min |
| **Fushimi Inari** | Thousand torii gates, shrine mountain | 90-120 min |
| **Hike** | First 20 min to lower overlook OR full summit (2-3 hours) | Variable |

**Pro tip:** Arrive by **6:00-6:30 AM** for empty torii gates. The shrine is magical in early light. By 8 AM crowds begin; by 10 AM it's packed. Trains start at 5:30 AM.

**Mid-Morning: Return + Breakfast**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Return to Kyoto** | JR from Inari Station | 5 min |
| **Breakfast** | Near Kyoto Station or hotel area | 30-45 min |

**Late Morning/Afternoon: Arashiyama**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **JR to Saga-Arashiyama** | JR Sagano Line (15 min) | 20 min |
| **Arashiyama Bamboo Grove** | Free, 24h access | 30-45 min |
| **Monkey Park Iwatayama** | Wild macaques, city views (800 yen, 20-min hike) | 60 min (optional) |
| **Lunch** | Yudofu (tofu) at Shoraian or similar | 45-60 min |
| **Togetsukyo Bridge** | Iconic river crossing | 15 min |

**Pro tip:** Bamboo Grove is best at **early morning (before 8 AM)** or late afternoon for fewer people and beautiful light filtering through.

**Late Afternoon: Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Bus to Kinkaku-ji** | Bus #12 or #59 from Kyoto Station | 40 min |
| **Kinkaku-ji** | Gold-leaf covered temple, pond reflection | 30-45 min |

**Pro tip:** Kinkaku-ji opens at 9:00 AM. Arrive right at opening for fewer crowds and better photos with morning light reflecting off the pond.

**Optional: Nijo Castle**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Bus/Metro to Nijojo-mae** | From Kinkaku-ji | 20 min |
| **Nijo Castle** | Nightingale floors, painted screens | 45-60 min |
| **Return to central Kyoto** | Bus or subway | 15-20 min |

**Evening:**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Dinner** | Menami (obanzai home-style, Sanjo Kiyamachi) | 60-90 min |

## Day 3 | Sep 7 -- Nara Day Trip

### Route: Deer Park, Great Buddha, Ancient Shrines

**Distance:** ~4 km walking in Nara | **Time:** 5-6 hours

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **9:00 AM Departure** | Kintetsu Kyoto Station | - |
| **Kintetsu Express to Nara** | 45 min to Kintetsu Nara Station | 45 min |
| **Nara Park** | 1,000+ semi-wild deer, buy crackers (Y200) | 45-60 min |
| **Todai-ji** | Great Buddha, world's largest wooden building (Y600) | 45-60 min |
| **Lunch in Nara** | Local restaurants near park | 45-60 min |
| **Kasuga Taisha** | Thousand lanterns shrine (inner Y500) | 30-45 min |
| **Kofuku-ji** | Historic temple (free grounds, Y700 treasure hall) | 20-30 min |
| **3:00-4:00 PM Return** | Kintetsu to Kyoto | 45 min |
| **Evening in Kyoto** | Matcha at Tsujiri Gion | 30 min |
| **Kaiseki dinner** | Kanga-an (vegetarian kaiseki + secret bar) | 90-120 min |

**Getting there:**
- **Kintetsu Express** from Kintetsu Kyoto Station: Y760 (~$5) one-way, 45 min
- Drops you at **Kintetsu Nara Station** -- right next to the deer park
- **Alternative:** JR Miyakoji Rapid (Y720, 45 min) to JR Nara Station (15-20 min walk to park)

**Nara Park tips:**
- Deer are wild but accustomed to humans -- they can be pushy when food is visible!
- Buy shika senbei (deer crackers) from vendors in the park
- Todai-ji houses the massive Daibutsu (Great Buddha) -- awe-inspiring
- Kasuga Taisha is famous for its hundreds of bronze and stone lanterns

**Evening back in Kyoto:**
- **Tsujiri Gion** -- matcha parfait or soft-serve (~Y700-1,500)
- **Kanga-an** -- Zen vegetarian kaiseki with secret bar after meal (reserve 3+ days ahead by phone)

## Day 4 | Sep 8 -- Free Day / Deeper Kyoto

### Route: Philosopher's Path → Nanzen-ji → Kitano Tenmangu → Ryoan-ji

**Distance:** ~6 km walking | **Time:** Full day

**Morning: Philosopher's Path**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Bus to Ginkaku-ji** | Bus #5 or #100 from Kyoto Station | 30 min |
| **Ginkaku-ji (Silver Pavilion)** | Zen temple, dry sand garden (Y500) | 30-45 min |
| **Philosopher's Path** | 2 km canal-side walk to Nanzen-ji | 45-60 min |
| **Nanzen-ji Temple** | Free grounds, massive sanmon gate | 45-60 min |

**Pro tip:** The Philosopher's Path is one of Kyoto's most beautiful walks. The 2 km path follows a canal lined with hundreds of cherry trees (scenic in any season). Allow time to stop at sub-temples along the way.

**Afternoon: Northern Kyoto**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Lunch** | Okazaki area | 45-60 min |
| **Bus to Kitano Tenmangu** | Bus #204 from central Kyoto | 20 min |
| **Kitano Tenmangu** | Scholars' shrine, plum garden | 30-45 min |
| **Bus to Ryoan-ji** | Short bus ride | 10 min |
| **Ryoan-ji Rock Garden** | Japan's most famous zen garden (Y500) | 30-45 min |
| **Return to central Kyoto** | Bus | 20 min |

**Pro tip:** Ryoan-ji's rock garden is a study in minimalism -- 15 rocks in a sea of raked gravel. Best experienced in quiet contemplation.

**Evening:**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Final Kyoto dinner** | Koyoken (obanzai, 9 seats, feels like home) | 90-120 min |

## Kyoto Transport Quick Reference

| Transport | Best For | Cost |
|-----------|----------|------|
| **City Bus** | Reaching temples not on subway | Flat Y230 within central zone |
| **Subway** | Quick cross-town trips | Y220-360 depending on distance |
| **JR Lines** | Arashiyama (Saga-Arashiyama), Nara (JR Nara Line) | Y170-240 typical |
| **Bicycle rental** | Philosopher's Path, Higashiyama, flat areas | Y1,000-1,500/day |
| **Taxi** | Late-night, groups of 3-4 | Starting Y620 for first 1.5 km |

**Day Passes:**
- **Bus & Subway 1-Day Pass:** Y1,100 -- good value for 4+ rides
- **IC Cards (ICOCA/Suica):** Prepaid, work on all transit

## Weather Considerations (Early September)

- **Temperature:** Highs 31-33°C (88-91°F), lows 22-24°C (72-75°F)
- **Humidity:** 70-80% -- hot and sticky
- **Rain:** ~43% chance of rain on any given day; typhoon season

**Strategy:**
- **Early starts** -- temples at opening (6-9 AM) avoid crowds and midday heat
- **Midday breaks** -- AC in restaurants, cafes, or shopping arcades
- **Late afternoon** -- many temples have beautiful evening light
- **Indoor backups** -- shopping malls (Kyoto Station, Takashiyama), museums
