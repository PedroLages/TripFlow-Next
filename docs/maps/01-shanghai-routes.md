# Shanghai Walking Routes
## Day-by-Day Neighborhood Guides (Aug 27-30, 2026)

## Day 1 | Aug 27 -- The Bund & French Concession

### Route: French Concession → The Bund

**Distance:** ~3.5 km walking | **Time:** 2-3 hours

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Start: Former French Concession** | Tree-lined avenues, colonial architecture, cafes | 45-60 min |
| Wukang Road | Historic mansions, "Broadway of Shanghai" | 20 min |
| Tianzifang | Artsy lanes, shikumen houses, galleries (optional) | 30-45 min |
| **Metro to Nanjing East Rd** | Skip long walk in August heat | 15 min |
| **The Bund (Waitan)** | 1.5 km waterfront promenade, Pudong skyline | 60-90 min |
| **End: Nanjing Road** | Dinner options, shopping |

**Walk details:**
1. **French Concession Start:** Begin at **South Shaanxi Road** Metro station. Walk along **Wukang Road** (Route Ferguson) for the best concentration of historic colonial architecture.
2. **Tianzifang Option:** If time/energy permits, take Metro to **Dapuqiao Station** for Tianzifang's artsy labyrinth.
3. **Metro to Bund:** From People's Square or South Shaanxi Rd, take Line 2 to **Nanjing East Road** (about 15-20 min total).
4. **Bund Walk:** Start at the **southern end** near Yan'an East Road and walk north toward the **Garden Bridge** (Waibaidu Bridge). This gives you the classic Pudong skyline view as you walk.

**Pro tip:** Time your Bund walk to arrive around **6:00 PM** for the light show. The skyline illuminates beautifully at dusk.

**Food along the way:**
- French Concession: Numerous brunch spots, Lost Heaven (Yunnan cuisine)
- The Bund: Xiao Shaoxing (cold drunken chicken, congee) on Yunnan Road nearby

## Day 2 | Aug 28 -- Zhujiajiao Water Town

### Route: Ancient Canals & Bridges

**Distance:** ~3 km walking | **Time:** 3-4 hours on-site

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Metro: Zhujiajiao Station** | Line 17 terminus | - |
| Walk to old town (15 min) | Through modern streets to historic area | 15 min |
| **Fangsheng Bridge** | Five-arch stone bridge (1571), iconic photo spot | 15 min |
| Canal-side streets | Traditional architecture, snack vendors | 60-90 min |
| **Kezhi Garden** | Qing Dynasty scholar garden (optional) | 30-45 min |
| Gondola ride | Canal perspective of the town | 20-30 min |
| Lunch | Canal-side restaurant | 45-60 min |

**Getting there:**
- **Metro Line 17** from Shanghai to Zhujiajiao Station (60-90 min depending on starting point)
- **Alternative:** Didi/taxi (~100-150 RMB one-way, 45-60 min)

**Walking route within Zhujiajiao:**
1. From metro, walk south on **Zhuxi Road** for 15 minutes to the old town entrance
2. Cross **Fangsheng Bridge** for the classic photo op
3. Wander the **north-south canal streets** -- mostly pedestrian-only
4. Stop at **Kezhi Garden** if interested in classical Chinese gardens (requires combo ticket)
5. Optional: Gondola boat ride (~80 RMB) from the wharf near Fangsheng Bridge

**What to eat:**
- Zongzi (glutinous rice dumplings) -- local specialty
- Braised pork knuckle
- Steamed freshwater fish

**Pro tip:** Go on **weekday** (Wed or Thu) to avoid crowds. Aug 27-30 are Wed-Sat, so aim for Wed or Thu.

## Day 3 | Aug 29 -- Yu Garden, Tianzifang, 1000 Trees

### Route: Old Shanghai → Artsy Lanes → Modern Architecture

**Distance:** ~5 km walking with metro segments | **Time:** Full day

**Morning: Yu Garden & Bazaar**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Yuyuan Garden Station** (Line 10) | Metro arrival | - |
| **Yu Garden** | Classical Ming garden, pavilions, ponds | 45-60 min |
| **Yuyuan Bazaar** | Traditional snack streets, souvenirs | 30-45 min |
| **Xiaolongbao lunch** | Nanxiang or Jia Jia Tang Bao | 30 min |

**Midday: Tianzifang**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Metro to Dapuqiao** | Line 9 from Yuyuan Garden | 20 min |
| **Tianzifang** | Shikumen lanes, art galleries, boutiques | 45-60 min |
| Lunch or coffee | Artsy cafes in the area | 30-45 min |

**Late Afternoon/Evening: 1000 Trees**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Metro to Jiangning Rd** | Line 13 from Dapuqiao | 15 min |
| **1000 Trees (Tian'an Qianshu)** | Heatherwick "mountain" building, Suzhou Creek views | 45-90 min |
| **Sunset** | Best lighting on the building | - |

**Walking route details:**

1. **Yu Garden (Morning):** Exit at Yuyuan Garden Station (Line 10). The garden entrance is a short walk. After the garden, explore the bazaar for street food and souvenirs.

2. **Tianzifang (Midday):** Take Line 9 to **Dapuqiao Station**. Tianzifang is a 5-minute walk. Wander the labyrinth of lanes filled with art, crafts, and cafes. It's a great spot for AC break and coffee.

3. **1000 Trees (Late Afternoon):** Take Line 13 to **Jiangning Road Station**, Exit 1. Walk ~8 minutes to 600 Moganshan Road. Explore the building exterior, rooftop terrace, and Suzhou Creek waterfront.

**Pro tip:** 1000 Trees is spectacular at **sunset** when the lighting illuminates the "mountain" silhouette. The building faces west, so late afternoon golden hour is ideal.

**Dinner options:**
- **Old Jesse (Lao Ji Shi)** -- Michelin Bib Gourmand, braised pork belly (Tianping Rd, French Concession area)
- **Zhaozhou Road** -- Late-night wontons, steamed buns, fried noodles (Laoximen Station)

## Weather Considerations (Late August)

- **Highs:** 33-35°C (91-95°F), can spike to 38°C (100°F)
- **Humidity:** 82-84% -- feels oppressive
- **Rain:** Thunderstorms common (~8-15 rainy days in August)

**Strategy:**
- Plan outdoor walking for **before 10 AM** or **after 5 PM**
- Use midday for indoor attractions, malls, or AC restaurants
- Carry compact umbrella daily
- Stay hydrated -- refillable water bottle recommended
