## Day 1 | Sep 15 -- Central Beijing

### Route: Arrival → Tiananmen Square → Forbidden City → Peking Duck

**Distance:** ~4 km walking | **Time:** 5-6 hours

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Arrival: PEK Airport** | Capital Airport | - |
| **Airport Express** | To Dongzhimen (20-30 min, CNY 25) | 30 min |
| **Check-in** | Hotel in Dongcheng district | - |
| **Tiananmen Square** | World's largest public square | 30-45 min |
| **Forbidden City** | Imperial palace, 5,000+ years history (CNY 60) | 180-240 min |
| **Exit north gate** | Into hutong area | - |
| **Peking Duck dinner** | Siji Minfu or Da Dong | 60-90 min |
| **Wangfujing Snack Street** | Evening stroll, street food | 30-45 min |
| **Late-night: Guijie (Ghost Street)** | Spicy crayfish, grilled fish | 60-90 min |

**Walking route details:**

1. **Tiananmen Square:** Enter from the south. Security screening required (bring passport). This is the world's largest public square, surrounded by important government buildings. Allow 30-45 minutes.

2. **Forbidden City:** Enter through the **Meridian Gate (south entrance)** -- only entry point. Exit through the **Gate of Divine Might (north exit)**. One-way flow. Allow 3-5 hours for a thorough visit.

**CRITICAL:** Forbidden City tickets must be booked **7 days in advance at 8:00 PM Beijing time** via https://gugong.ktmtech.cn/. For Sep 15 visit, book on Sep 8 at 8 PM.

3. **Peking Duck Dinner:**
   - **Siji Minfu** (Best value-to-quality): CNY 198-268 (~$27-37) for whole duck. Popular, refined.
   - **Da Dong** (Modern, lighter): CNY 398 (~$55). Crispy translucent skin. Smaller portions.

4. **Wangfujing Snack Street:** Evening stroll through touristy but atmospheric food street. Expect: scorpion-on-a-stick (gimmick), candied hawthorn, lamb skewers, stinky tofu.

5. **Guijie (Ghost Street):** Late-night alternative to Wangfujing. Less touristy, more local. Famous for spicy crayfish and grilled fish in chili broth. Dongzhimen area, walking distance from Dongzhimen metro. Open till 2-4 AM.

## Day 2 | Sep 16 -- Great Wall Day

### Route: Beijing → Mutianyu Great Wall → Beijing

**Distance:** ~5 km hiking on the wall | **Time:** 8-10 hours total

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **8:00 AM Departure** | Dongzhimen (Mubus pickup) | - |
| **Mubus to Mutianyu** | 2 hr drive (CNY 80 round trip) | 120 min |
| **Cable car up** | To wall (CNY 120 round trip) | 10 min |
| **Hike the Great Wall** | 3-5 hours, 5.4 km walkable wall | 180-300 min |
| **Toboggan ride down** | Thrilling descent (included in cable car) | 10 min |
| **Lunch at the wall** | CNY 50-80 (~$7-11) | 45 min |
| **Return bus** | To Beijing (Mubus) | 120 min |
| **5:30-6:00 PM Arrival** | Dongzhimen, Beijing | - |

**Getting to Mutianyu:**

| Option | Time | Cost | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-------|
| **Mubus (recommended)** | 2 hr each way | CNY 80 (~$11) RT | Departs Dongzhimen 9 & 10 AM daily |
| **Public Bus 916 + transfer** | 2-3 hr | CNY 40-65 (~$6-9) | Budget option, slower |
| **Bus 867 (seasonal)** | 2.5 hr | CNY 16 (~$2.20) | Direct, Dongzhimen, Apr-Nov only |
| **Didi/Private car** | 1.5-2 hr | CNY 300-500 (~$40-70) RT | Most flexible |
| **Guided tour** | Full day | CNY 300-600+ (~$40-80) | Includes guide + lunch |

**Mubus booking:** Book via WeChat "mubus" or www.beijingmubus.cn. Reserve a day ahead.

**Hiking the wall:**
- Mutianyu has **5.4 km of walkable wall** -- well-restored, authentic feel
- Less crowded than Badaling
- Cable car up, hike east or west, then **toboggan down** (thrilling!)
- Bring water, sun protection, comfortable shoes

**Evening in Beijing:**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Hutong rickshaw tour** | Shichahai/Houhai Lake area (CNY 100-200) | 45-60 min |
| **Dinner: Pang Mei Noodles** | Chongqing spicy beef noodles, ice jelly | 30-45 min |

**Pang Mei Noodles:** Michelin Bib Gourmand. Xiang'er Hutong, Dongcheng. Chongqing spicy beef noodles. Start with medium spice. Ice jelly dessert is essential after the heat.

## Day 3 | Sep 17 -- Temples, Palace, and Culture

### Route: Temple of Heaven → Summer Palace → Art District OR Hutongs

**Distance:** ~4 km walking | **Time:** Full day

**Early Morning: Temple of Heaven**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **6:00 AM Departure** | Early for tai chi scene | - |
| **Temple of Heaven** | Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests (CNY 45) | 90-120 min |
| **Tai chi watching** | Locals doing morning exercises | 30-45 min |
| **Breakfast: Jianbing** | From street vendor (CNY 8-15) | 15 min |

**Pro tip:** Go at **6-8 AM** to see locals doing tai chi, dancing, and singing -- an unforgettable cultural experience. The park opens at 6 AM even though buildings open later (8 AM).

**Midday: Summer Palace**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Subway to Beigongmen** | Line 4 from Tiantandongmen | 30 min |
| **Summer Palace** | Kunming Lake, Longevity Hill, Long Corridor | 120-180 min |
| **Lunch near Summer Palace** | CNY 50-100 (~$7-14) | 45 min |

**Summer Palace highlights:** Kunming Lake, Longevity Hill, Long Corridor (728m of painted scenes), Marble Boat. Optional: Dragon boat across the lake.

**Late Afternoon/Evening: Choose One**

**Option A: 798 Art District**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Subway/Wangjing South** | Line 14/15, then taxi/bus | 45 min |
| **798 Art District** | Former factory, galleries, street art | 120-180 min |
| **Dinner nearby** | Various restaurants | 60 min |

**Option B: Nanluoguxiang Hutongs**

| Stop | What to See | Time Needed |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Subway to Nanluoguxiang** | Line 6 or 8 | 20 min |
| **Hutong exploration** | Traditional alleyways, shops, cafes | 90-120 min |
| **Souvenir shopping** | Local crafts, gifts | 60 min |
| **Dinner** | Hot pot or local restaurant | 60-90 min |

**Farewell Dinner Options:**

| Restaurant | Cuisine | Price/Person |
|------------|---------|--------------|
| **Yangdaye** | Lamb hot pot (Michelin-starred, nose-to-tail) | ~CNY 180 (~$25) |
| **Haidilao** | Hot pot chain, excellent service | CNY 80-150 (~$11-21) |
| **Donglaishun** | Traditional Beijing-style hot pot | CNY 80-120 (~$11-17) |

**Yangdaye:** Maizidian West St, Chaoyang. Reserve ahead. Ask staff for recommended cooking order for the 20+ fresh-cut lamb parts.

## Hutong Areas to Explore

| Area | Best For | Vibe |
|------|----------|------|
| **Nanluoguxiang** | Tourist-friendly shopping, cafes, bars | Hipster, bustling |
| **Shichahai/Houhai** | Lakeside, rickshaw tours, nightlife | Scenic, lively |
| **Qianmen** | Historic, near Tiananmen, shopping | Traditional, commercial |
| **Dongcheng** | Peking duck, local life | Authentic, central |

**Hutong tips:**
- Explore on foot for free
- Rickshaw tours: CNY 100-200 (~$14-28), negotiate price before boarding
- Many hidden restaurants and bars in narrow alleys
- Best times: Morning for local life, evening for atmosphere

## Weather Considerations (Mid-September)

- **Temperature:** Highs 26-30°C (79-86°F), lows 16-20°C (61-68°F)
- **Conditions:** Mostly clear, dry, comfortable
- **Rainfall:** ~46mm across ~8 rainy days for the whole month
- **Air quality:** September is the **best month** for AQI in Beijing (avg 56, moderate)

**What to pack:**
- Light layers for temperature variation
- Light jacket for evenings
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Sunscreen
- Light rain jacket (just in case)
