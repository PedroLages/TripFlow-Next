# Packing List
## 22-Night Asia Trip | Aug 27 - Sep 18, 2026

Route: Shanghai (3N) -> Hong Kong (3N) -> Osaka (3N) -> Kyoto (4N) -> Tokyo (6N) -> Beijing (3N)

Weather summary: Hot and humid throughout (25-35C / 77-95F), typhoon season in Shanghai/Hong Kong/Osaka, gradual cooling toward Beijing (16-30C, dry). Pack light, breathable, and rain-ready.

**Laundry plan:** Do laundry 3 times during the trip -- once in Hong Kong (Day 4-5), once in Kyoto (Day 10-11), and once in Tokyo (Day 15-16). This means you need roughly 5-6 days of clothing at any given time.

---

## 1. Clothing

### Tops (6 total)
- [ ] 4 moisture-wicking crew/V-neck tees (merino wool or synthetic blend -- dries overnight, resists odor)
- [ ] 1 lightweight linen or linen-blend button-up shirt (doubles as "nicer" layer for restaurants)
- [ ] 1 collared polo or smart casual short-sleeve shirt (for kaiseki dinner or fine dining)

### Bottoms (4 total)
- [ ] 2 quick-dry travel pants (convertible zip-off or slim joggers -- choose a neutral color that works for both day exploring and casual dinners)
- [ ] 1 pair lightweight shorts (for theme parks, casual days, Great Wall hike)
- [ ] 1 pair chinos or smart-casual pants (for kaiseki / fine dining evenings -- dark color hides stains)

### Underwear & Socks (6 each)
- [ ] 6 moisture-wicking boxer briefs (ExOfficio or similar quick-dry -- wash a pair every other night if needed)
- [ ] 6 pairs no-show or ankle socks (merino wool blend -- critical because you remove shoes frequently at temples and ryokans in Japan)

### Outerwear & Layers (3 total)
- [ ] 1 packable rain jacket with hood (Gore-Tex or similar waterproof/breathable -- non-negotiable for typhoon season; doubles as wind layer)
- [ ] 1 lightweight hoodie or zip-up fleece (for aggressive AC on trains, malls, and restaurants -- it will be cold inside)
- [ ] 1 ultralight down or synthetic puffy jacket (packable, for Beijing evenings when temps drop to 16C/61F and for over-AC'd Shinkansen rides)

### Footwear (3 pairs)
- [ ] 1 pair broken-in walking shoes (trail runners or supportive sneakers -- this is your primary shoe for 15-20k steps/day and the Great Wall hike)
- [ ] 1 pair lightweight sandals with back strap (Teva, Chaco, or Birkenstock -- for evening walks, hostels, and easy on/off at Japanese temples)
- [ ] 1 pair no-show slip-on shoes or hotel slippers (for flights and quick temple visits -- optional if sandals cover this)

### Accessories
- [ ] 1 wide-brim hat or baseball cap (sun protection is critical at 33-35C)
- [ ] 1 buff/neck gaiter (sun protection, dust shield on Great Wall, face covering on Beijing smog days)
- [ ] 1 pair polarized sunglasses
- [ ] 1 compact travel umbrella (keep in day pack at all times -- afternoon thunderstorms are near-daily in late August)
- [ ] 1 packable tote bag or drawstring bag (for temple shoe storage, market shopping, laundry runs)

### Sleepwear
- [ ] 1 lightweight t-shirt and shorts for sleeping (most accommodations have AC, but some ryokans provide yukata)

## 2. Electronics & Tech

### Core Devices
- [ ] Smartphone (with eSIMs pre-installed for China, Hong Kong, and Japan)
- [ ] Laptop or tablet (optional -- only if you need to work or edit photos; skip to save weight)
- [ ] Mirrorless camera or compact camera (optional -- phone cameras are excellent now; bring only if photography is a priority)
- [ ] Camera charger + spare battery (if bringing camera)
- [ ] Earbuds or headphones (noise-canceling recommended for flights and 8+ hour trains)

### Power & Charging
- [ ] 1 universal travel adapter with USB-C and USB-A ports (covers all 3 countries; see plug note below)
- [ ] 1 USB-C to USB-C cable (for phone fast charging)
- [ ] 1 USB-C to Lightning cable (if applicable)
- [ ] 1 USB-A cable (backup / camera charging)
- [ ] 1 portable power bank, 20,000mAh minimum (heavy phone use for maps, translation, payments -- you will drain your battery daily)
- [ ] 1 compact multi-port USB charger (Anker Nano or similar -- charge phone + power bank + earbuds simultaneously from one outlet)

### Plug Types by Country
| Country | Plug Type | Compatible with US plugs? |
|---------|-----------|--------------------------|
| China | Type A (US-style 2-prong) + Type I (angled 3-prong) | Yes -- US 2-prong fits directly |
| Japan | Type A (US-style 2-prong, 100V) | Yes -- US 2-prong fits directly |
| Hong Kong | Type G (British 3-prong) | No -- adapter required |

**Bottom line:** You only strictly need an adapter for Hong Kong. A universal adapter covers everything and removes guesswork. Most modern chargers (phone, laptop) handle 100-240V, so no voltage converter needed.

### Storage
- [ ] 1-2 spare microSD cards or USB-C thumb drive (for photo backup)

## 3. Travel Documents & Money

### Documents
- [ ] Passport (valid 6+ months beyond Sep 18, 2026 -- check expiration NOW)
- [ ] China visa or 240-hour transit-without-visa confirmation (verify eligibility for your nationality)
- [ ] Japan visa (if required for your nationality; many countries get visa-free entry up to 90 days)
- [ ] 2 color photocopies of passport info page (keep one in luggage, one in day pack -- separate from actual passport)
- [ ] Digital scan of passport saved to phone and cloud storage (Google Drive, iCloud, etc.)
- [ ] Travel insurance policy printout and digital copy (include 24-hour emergency number)
- [ ] Hotel booking confirmations (printed or saved offline -- Chinese immigration may ask for your hotel address)
- [ ] Flight itineraries for all legs (printed or saved offline)
- [ ] 2 extra passport-sized photos (useful for unexpected visa/permit situations)

### Money & Cards
- [ ] 2 credit cards from different networks (Visa + Mastercard -- always have a backup; notify banks of travel dates)
- [ ] 1 debit card for ATM cash withdrawals (check foreign transaction fees; Schwab or similar no-fee card is ideal)
- [ ] $100-150 USD in cash (emergency reserve -- universally exchangeable)
- [ ] CNY 500-800 (~$70-110 USD) in cash (get from bank before departure or at Shanghai airport ATM; some street vendors are cash-only despite Alipay dominance)
- [ ] HKD 500-800 (~$65-100 USD) in cash (for Octopus card top-up, small vendors, taxis)
- [ ] JPY 30,000-40,000 (~$200-270 USD) in cash (Japan is still heavily cash-based; withdraw more from 7-Eleven ATMs as needed)
- [ ] Slim RFID-blocking wallet or money belt (pickpocketing is rare but crowded metro systems warrant caution)

### Payment Setup (Do Before Departure)
- [ ] Alipay set up with international card linked and passport verified
- [ ] WeChat Pay set up as backup payment method for China
- [ ] Octopus card (buy at HK airport on arrival, or use Apple Pay Octopus if supported)
- [ ] Suica/ICOCA IC card loaded on phone (Welcome Suica app for iPhone, physical card from station for Android)

## 4. Toiletries & Health

### Toiletries (travel sizes -- refill from convenience stores as needed)
- [ ] Toothbrush + toothpaste
- [ ] Deodorant (hard to find in Asia -- bring your own full-size)
- [ ] Razor + spare blades
- [ ] Shampoo + conditioner (solid bars save space and avoid liquid limits)
- [ ] Face wash
- [ ] Lip balm with SPF
- [ ] Contact lenses + solution (if applicable; bring backup glasses)
- [ ] Small microfiber towel (for budget accommodations or onsen; many hotels provide towels)

### Sun & Heat Protection (Critical)
- [ ] Sunscreen SPF 50+, PA++++ (apply every 2 hours; Japanese sunscreens like Biore UV Aqua Rich are excellent and available at any Japanese convenience store or drugstore)
- [ ] Aloe vera gel, small tube (for inevitable sunburn)
- [ ] Cooling towel or bandana (soak in water, drape on neck -- lifesaver at 35C)
- [ ] Portable handheld fan (battery-operated or USB rechargeable -- sold everywhere in Asia, but bring one to be ready on Day 1)

### Medications & First Aid
- [ ] Any daily prescription medications (bring full supply + copy of prescription)
- [ ] Ibuprofen or acetaminophen (pain/fever)
- [ ] Imodium (stomach issues from unfamiliar food)
- [ ] Pepto-Bismol tablets (nausea, indigestion)
- [ ] Antihistamine (Benadryl or Zyrtec -- for allergic reactions, bug bites, helps with sleep on flights)
- [ ] Electrolyte packets, 6-8 (Liquid IV, Drip Drop -- critical for rehydration in extreme heat)
- [ ] Band-aids and blister pads (Compeed or moleskin -- 15-20k steps/day will test your feet)
- [ ] Small tube of antibiotic ointment (Neosporin)
- [ ] Anti-chafing balm or powder (Body Glide -- hot, humid, lots of walking; you will thank yourself)
- [ ] Insect repellent with DEET or picaridin (mosquitoes are active in late summer across all cities)
- [ ] Hand sanitizer, small bottle (refill as needed; also useful before street food)
- [ ] Tissues / wet wipes (many public restrooms in China do not provide toilet paper)
- [ ] Motion sickness tablets (if prone -- useful for winding mountain roads to Great Wall or Hakone)

## 5. Apps & Digital Prep

Pre-download and set up all of the following before departure. Several China apps must be configured outside of China, and VPN must be installed before arrival (it cannot be downloaded from Chinese app stores).

### China (Shanghai + Beijing)
- [ ] **Alipay** -- payments everywhere; link international card + verify passport identity
- [ ] **WeChat** -- messaging and backup payment; Chinese contacts may only use this
- [ ] **Didi** -- ride-hailing (China's Uber equivalent)
- [ ] **Gaode Maps (Amap)** -- navigation in China (Google Maps is blocked and unreliable)
- [ ] **Pleco** -- Chinese-English dictionary with handwriting recognition
- [ ] **VPN app** (ExpressVPN, Astrill, or similar) -- install and test BEFORE entering China; Google, WhatsApp, Instagram, and most Western apps are blocked

### Japan (Osaka + Kyoto + Tokyo)
- [ ] **Welcome Suica Mobile** -- IC transit card for iPhone
- [ ] **SmartEX** -- book Shinkansen bullet train tickets (Kyoto -> Tokyo)
- [ ] **USJ app** -- Universal Studios Japan wait times and timed entry
- [ ] **Fuji-Q Highland app** -- ride wait times and park info
- [ ] **Google Translate** -- download offline packs for Japanese and Chinese before departure

### Hong Kong
- [ ] **Octopus app** -- check balance, top up (or use physical card)
- [ ] **Google Maps** -- works normally in Hong Kong (unlike mainland China)

### General
- [ ] **Google Translate** offline language packs: Simplified Chinese, Traditional Chinese, Japanese
- [ ] **Airalo / Saily** -- eSIM management for data plans
- [ ] **XE Currency** or similar -- quick currency conversion between CNY, HKD, JPY, USD
- [ ] **Citymapper or Navitime** -- detailed transit routing in major Asian cities
- [ ] **Offline maps** downloaded in Google Maps for all 6 cities (backup for connectivity gaps)
- [ ] **Trip.com or Ctrip** -- backup booking platform widely used in Asia

## 7. Packing Tips & Strategy

### Bag Recommendations
- **Main bag:** 40-45L carry-on-sized backpack or rolling carry-on (aim to go carry-on only if possible -- you are moving between 6 cities and will deeply regret checking luggage at every transfer)
- **Day pack:** 20-25L packable backpack (lives inside main bag on travel days, comes out daily for sightseeing)
- **Optional:** Small packing cube or dry bag for dirty laundry separation

### Compression & Organization
- [ ] 4-5 packing cubes (separate: tops, bottoms, underwear/socks, outerwear, misc)
- [ ] 1 shoe bag (keep dirty soles off clean clothes)
- [ ] 2-3 ziplock bags, gallon-size (wet swimwear, leaky toiletries, dirty laundry overflow)
- [ ] 1 hanging toiletry bag (quick access in small hotel bathrooms)
- [ ] 1 slim document organizer or passport wallet

### Laundry Strategy (3 Washes Over 22 Nights)

| Wash # | When | Where | Method |
|--------|------|-------|--------|
| 1 | Day 4-5 (Hong Kong) | Hotel laundry or coin laundromat | Wash everything worn Days 1-4 |
| 2 | Day 10-11 (Kyoto) | Coin laundry (common near budget hotels and ryokans in Japan) | Wash everything worn Days 5-10 |
| 3 | Day 15-16 (Tokyo) | Coin laundry (extremely easy to find in Tokyo) | Wash everything worn Days 11-15 |

**Coin laundry costs in Japan:** Roughly $2-4 per wash, $1-2 per dry cycle. Takes about 60-90 minutes total. Many run 24/7.

**Backup plan:** Sink-wash merino and quick-dry items overnight. Hang in bathroom or over AC unit. Most items dry by morning in humid heat (merino wool and synthetic blends dry fastest).

### Weight & Space Targets
- **Total packed weight goal:** Under 10kg / 22lbs (excluding day pack contents)
- **Heaviest items:** Walking shoes (wear on travel days, never pack), rain jacket, power bank
- **Wear your bulkiest outfit on flight days** to save bag space (walking shoes, long pants, hoodie)

### What NOT to Pack
- Jeans (too heavy, too hot, takes forever to dry)
- Cotton t-shirts (absorb sweat, stay wet, breed odor)
- Full-size toiletries (buy locally at convenience stores -- 7-Eleven and FamilyMart are everywhere)
- Towels (every hotel provides them; bring microfiber only if doing hostels)
- Guidebooks (your phone replaces all of these)
- Fancy dress shoes (your smart casual outfit works with clean walking shoes or sandals)
- Excessive souvenirs anxiety (ship heavy items home via post office -- Japan Post is reliable and affordable)
