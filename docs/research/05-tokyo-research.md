# Tokyo Travel Research: 6 Nights (Sep 9-15, 2026)

## 1. Getting Around Tokyo

### The Yamanote Line (Your Best Friend)
The JR Yamanote Line is a 34 km loop through central Tokyo connecting **30 stations** including all the major hubs: Shinjuku, Shibuya, Harajuku, Ikebukuro, Ueno, Akihabara, Tokyo Station, and Shinagawa. A full loop takes about 60 minutes. Trains run every 2.5 minutes during rush hour. It is operated by JR East, so a JR Pass covers it, and Suica/Pasmo IC cards work on it too.

**Key station-to-station times on Yamanote:**
- Shinjuku to Shibuya: ~7 min
- Shinjuku to Akihabara: ~20 min
- Shinjuku to Tokyo Station: ~15 min
- Shibuya to Harajuku: ~2 min

### Tokyo Metro & Toei Subway
Tokyo has **13 subway lines** (9 Tokyo Metro + 4 Toei). These fill in the gaps between JR stations and reach places the Yamanote Line does not. A **Tokyo Metro 24-hour pass** costs only 800 yen (~$5-6 USD) and can be good value on heavy subway days.

### Suica / Pasmo IC Cards
- **Functionally identical** -- both work on all JR lines, Metro, Toei, buses, and even convenience stores/vending machines
- **Suica** is issued by JR East; **Pasmo** by non-JR operators
- **Welcome Suica Mobile** (app): Set up on your phone before the trip. Valid for 180 days, no 500 yen deposit, no airport queue. Recommended.
- **Physical card**: Available at Narita/Haneda airports and major JR stations. Requires a 500 yen refundable deposit.
- Load with cash at any station kiosk or via the app

## 2. Must-See Attractions

### Fuji-Q Highland
**Why it's special:** Amusement park at the base of **Mt. Fuji** with world-class roller coasters and stunning mountain views. Famous for record-breaking thrill rides. Free park entry -- you only pay for rides.

**Getting There from Shinjuku:**
| Option | Time | Cost (One-Way) | Notes |
|--------|------|----------------|-------|
| **Highway Bus** | ~1h 40m | ~2,200 yen ($15) | Cheapest; departs Shinjuku Expressway Bus Terminal |
| **Q-Pack (bus + pass combo)** | ~1h 40m | ~10,000 yen ($67) total | Best value -- includes RT bus + 1-Day Free Pass |
| **Fuji Excursion Train** | ~2h | ~4,130 yen ($28) one-way | Most comfortable; direct from JR Shinjuku. Book ahead |

**Ticket Prices:**
| Type | Cost | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Park entry | Free | Walk around, shop, and dine for free |
| 1-Day Free Pass (unlimited rides) | 6,000-7,800 yen (~$40-52) | Dynamic pricing; higher on weekends |
| Priority Pass (per ride) | ~1,000 yen (~$7) | Skip the line; limited daily availability |

**Top Rides:**
1. **Eejanaika** -- 4th-dimension coaster with seats that rotate 360 degrees independently. 14 inversions (world record tied). The most intense ride in the park.
2. **Takabisha** -- features a 121-degree beyond-vertical drop (one of the steepest in the world). Multiple inversions.
3. **Fujiyama** -- classic 79m-tall steel coaster with incredible Mt. Fuji views during the ride. A park icon since 1996.
4. **Do-Dodonpa** -- **PERMANENTLY CLOSED** (shut down 2024 due to injuries). Do not plan for this ride.

**Other Attractions:**
- **Fujiyama Tower** -- 55m observation tower with panoramic Mt. Fuji views
- **Shining Flower Ferris Wheel** -- 50m tall, 12-minute ride with unobstructed Fuji views
- **Naruto / Boruto Fuji Hidden Leaf Village** -- anime-themed area with attractions and photo ops

**Strategy:**
- **Arrive before park opening** (8:30 AM on Saturdays). Lines form early.
- **Buy Priority Passes immediately** via the Fuji-Q app for Eejanaika and Takabisha -- they sell out on busy days.
- **Ride order:** Takabisha or Eejanaika first (longest waits), then Fujiyama, then observation/Ferris wheel in the afternoon.
- **Download the Fuji-Q Highland app** for real-time wait times and Priority Pass purchases.
- **Weather warning:** Rain or high winds will close coasters. Check the forecast and have a backup Tokyo plan ready.
- Budget a **full day** (8-10 hours including travel).

**Food Inside the Park:**
- **Food Stadium** -- main food court with multiple cuisines (~1,000-2,000 yen/meal)
- **Fujiyama Burger Shop** -- park-exclusive themed burgers
- **Central Park lawn** -- grab takeout and eat with Mt. Fuji views

**Mt. Fuji Views:**
Best spots for photos: Fujiyama Tower, Ferris wheel, and Central Park lawn. Morning offers the best visibility before afternoon clouds roll in. September has decent but not guaranteed Fuji views.

### Akihabara
**Best for:** Anime, manga, retro gaming, electronics, maid cafes, otaku culture.

**Key Shops & Experiences:**
- **Super Potato** -- legendary retro gaming store. Multiple floors of vintage consoles, cartridges, and a retro arcade on the top floor. A must for gamers.
- **Radio Kaikan** -- iconic multi-floor building with 30+ specialty shops (K-Books, Kaiyodo, Volks) selling figures, trading cards, models, and manga.
- **Animate** -- Japan's largest anime goods chain. Massive Akihabara flagship.
- **Mandarake Complex** -- 8 floors of used manga, anime goods, cosplay items, and doujinshi.
- **Yodobashi Camera Akihabara** -- enormous electronics megastore (tax-free floor available).
- **Maid Cafes** -- @Home Cafe is the most famous and has English-speaking maids. Expect a cover charge (~700 yen) plus food/drink orders. Quirky, fun experience.
- **Retro Arcades** -- Several buildings with crane games, rhythm games, and classic arcade cabinets.

**Tips:**
- Explore the **back streets** off Chuo-dori for hidden gems and smaller specialty shops.
- Many shops open at 10-11 AM; maid cafes and arcades run into the late evening.
- Budget 3-5 hours for a thorough visit.
- **Access:** JR Yamanote Line to Akihabara Station (Electric Town exit).

### Shibuya Crossing & Area
- The world's busiest pedestrian crossing. Best viewed from the **Shibuya Sky** observation deck (2,000 yen) or the Starbucks in Shibuya Tsutaya (2nd floor, free with drink purchase).
- **Hachiko statue** is right outside the station -- quick photo op.
- Surrounding area has excellent shopping (Shibuya 109, Parco, Hikarie).

### Shinjuku
- **Golden Gai** -- labyrinth of ~200 tiny bars in six narrow alleys. Most seat 6-10 people. Some have cover charges (300-1,000 yen). Look for bars with English signs or "Tourists Welcome" if you're nervous about language.
- **Kabukicho** -- Tokyo's famous entertainment/nightlife district. Neon-lit, lively. Home to Robot Restaurant (if still running) and Godzilla Head on the Toho building.
- **Shinjuku Gyoen** -- beautiful national garden, 500 yen entry. A peaceful escape.

### Harajuku / Takeshita Street
- **Takeshita Street** -- pedestrian street packed with trendy fashion shops, crepe stands, and quirky stores. Extremely crowded on weekends.
- Walk from there to **Cat Street** for more upscale/indie fashion.
- **Meiji Shrine** is a 5-minute walk from Harajuku Station -- a serene forested Shinto shrine. Free entry. Best visited in the morning.

### Tsukiji Outer Market
- The inner wholesale market moved to Toyosu, but the **outer market** remains and thrives with ~400 shops and stalls.
- Go for: fresh sushi, tamagoyaki (sweet egg), grilled seafood on sticks, fresh uni, tuna donburi.
- **Best time:** Early morning (7-10 AM). Many stalls close by early afternoon.
- Sushizanmai has a conveyor belt branch right in the market.

### Asakusa / Senso-ji Temple
- Tokyo's oldest temple (founded 628 AD). The massive red **Kaminarimon** gate is iconic.
- **Nakamise-dori** -- the shopping street leading to the temple, lined with souvenir and snack stalls.
- Best visited **early morning** before crowds, or **evening** when the temple is beautifully lit.
- Free entry.

### teamLab Borderless (Azabudai Hills)
- **Location:** Azabudai Hills Garden Plaza B, B1, Minato-ku. 2-minute walk from Kamiyacho Station (Hibiya Line).
- **Tickets:** Adults 3,800-4,800 yen; Children (4-12) 1,500 yen. **Book online in advance** -- it sells out.
- Allow 2-3 hours. Wear white clothing for better photo effects.

### Tokyo Tower vs. Tokyo Skytree
| | Tokyo Tower | Tokyo Skytree |
|--|------------|---------------|
| Height | 333m | 634m (tallest tower in the world) |
| Main deck | 150m, ~1,200 yen | 350m, ~2,100 yen |
| Top deck | 250m, ~3,000 yen | 450m, ~3,100 yen |
| Vibe | Classic, retro, romantic at night | Modern, higher views, more touristy |
| Access | Akabanebashi/Kamiyacho stations | Tokyo Skytree Station (Tobu) or Oshiage (Metro) |

**Recommendation:** Skytree for the views; Tokyo Tower for the vibes (especially at night).

## 3. Hakone Day Trip

### Hakone Free Pass (2-Day)
| | From Shinjuku | From Odawara |
|--|--------------|--------------|
| 2-day | 6,100 yen | 5,000 yen |
| 3-day | 6,500 yen | 5,400 yen |

**What it covers (unlimited use):**
- Odakyu Line (Shinjuku to Odawara, local trains only)
- Hakone Tozan Railway (Odawara to Gora)
- Hakone Tozan Cable Car (Gora to Sounzan)
- Hakone Ropeway (Sounzan to Togendai)
- Hakone Sightseeing Cruise/Pirate Ship (Lake Ashi)
- Hakone Tozan Bus & Odakyu Hakone Highway Bus (within the free area)

**NOT included:** Romancecar surcharge (1,200 yen each way, or 1,150 yen with e-ticket). Total round trip Romancecar surcharge: ~2,400 yen.

### The Classic Loop Course (Recommended Itinerary)

**Total cost:** Hakone Free Pass (6,100 yen) + Romancecar round trip (2,400 yen) = **~8,500 yen ($57 USD)**

**Route:**
1. **Shinjuku** -> take Romancecar to **Hakone-Yumoto** (85 min, scenic, reserved seats)
2. **Hakone-Yumoto** -> Tozan Railway switchback train to **Gora** (40 min, steep mountain climb)
3. **Gora** -> Cable car to **Sounzan** (10 min)
4. **Sounzan** -> Ropeway over volcanic valley (Owakudani) to **Togendai** (30 min). Stop at **Owakudani** for volcanic hot springs and the famous black eggs (said to add 7 years to your life, 500 yen for 5 eggs).
5. **Togendai** -> Pirate ship across **Lake Ashi** to **Moto-Hakone** (30 min). Mt. Fuji views from the lake (weather permitting).
6. **Moto-Hakone** -> Bus back to **Hakone-Yumoto** (35 min)
7. **Hakone-Yumoto** -> Romancecar back to **Shinjuku** (85 min)

**Optional stop:** Hakone Open Air Museum (Gora area, 1,600 yen admission). Excellent outdoor sculpture park with Picasso collection.

### Duration Needed
- **Minimum:** 8-9 hours (leave Shinjuku by 8 AM, return by 5-6 PM)
- **Comfortable:** 10-12 hours (leave by 7:30 AM, return by 7 PM)
- Book Romancecar in advance at Odakyu's website or at Shinjuku Station.

### Mt. Fuji Visibility in September
**Honest assessment: Low to moderate chances (~30-40%).**
- September is part of the worst visibility window (June-September) due to heat haze, humidity, and typhoon season clouds.
- Cloud cover builds throughout the day. **Best chance: early morning before 9 AM.**
- Even on "clear" days, the mountain can be hidden behind haze.
- Don't plan the trip *solely* for Fuji views -- enjoy the loop course itself (ropeway, pirate ship, Owakudani, hot springs). Fuji is a bonus.
- If Fuji visibility is a top priority, October-February is significantly better.

## 4. Food Guide

### Ramen
| Shop | Style | Location | Price | Wait |
|------|-------|----------|-------|------|
| **Ichiran** | Tonkotsu (Hakata-style) | Multiple locations (Shibuya, Shinjuku, etc.) | ~980-1,480 yen | 15-45 min |
| **Fuunji** | Tsukemen (dipping noodles) | Shinjuku (south exit) | ~900-1,100 yen | 30-60 min |

- **Ichiran** is famous for its individual booth seating ("Taste Concentration Counter") where you customize everything (broth richness, noodle firmness, garlic level, spice). Touristy but genuinely good tonkotsu. Popular with solo diners and women.
- **Fuunji** serves thick tsukemen noodles with a rich tonkotsu-gyokai (pork + fish) dipping broth. Widely considered one of Tokyo's best tsukemen shops. Expect a queue snaking across the street. Worth the wait.
- **Tip:** Ramen shops use ticket vending machines (push-button) at the entrance. Choose your bowl, pay, hand the ticket to staff.

### Sushi Tiers
| Tier | What to Expect | Price per Person | Where |
|------|---------------|-----------------|-------|
| **Conveyor Belt (Kaiten)** | Fun, casual, grab plates off the belt or order on tablet | 1,500-3,000 yen | Sushiro, Kura Sushi, Sushizanmai (Tsukiji) |
| **Standing Sushi** | Counter service, very fresh, quick | 2,000-4,000 yen | Scattered throughout transit hubs |
| **Mid-range Sushi** | Omakase-style at the counter, chef's choice | 5,000-10,000 yen | Tsukiji area, Ginza side streets |
| **High-end Omakase** | Multi-course chef's tasting, reservation required months ahead | 15,000-50,000+ yen | Ginza (Sukiyabashi Jiro etc.) |

**Recommendation for this trip:** Conveyor belt for fun + Tsukiji Outer Market for freshness = best value-to-quality ratio. Save omakase for a splurge night (~10,000-15,000 yen gets an excellent experience).

### Izakaya
Japanese pub-restaurants. Order rounds of small shared plates with drinks.
- **What to order:** Edamame, karaage (fried chicken), dashimaki tamago (rolled omelette), grilled fish, potato salad, pickled vegetables, beer/highball/sake.
- **Budget:** 2,000-4,000 yen per person including drinks.
- Good chains for beginners: Torikizoku (280 yen per item!), Ippudo.
- For atmosphere: **Golden Gai** (Shinjuku) or **Yurakucho** (under the train tracks).

### Yakitori in Yurakucho
The stretch of izakayas and yakitori joints **under the JR train tracks** between Yurakucho and Shimbashi stations is a quintessential Tokyo experience. Smoke, grilled chicken skewers, cold beer, salarymen unwinding after work. Peak atmosphere is weekday evenings (6-9 PM). No reservations needed -- just find an open stool. Budget ~2,000-3,000 yen for skewers + beer.

### Depachika (Department Store Basements)
The basement food floors of major department stores are a feast for the eyes and stomach.
- **What to find:** Beautifully packaged sweets, bento boxes, wagyu, fresh pastries, pickles, deli items, fruit, sake.
- **Best depachika:** Isetan Shinjuku (B1), Takashimaya (Nihonbashi or Shinjuku), Mitsukoshi Ginza, Daimaru Tokyo Station.
- **Tip:** Go 30-60 minutes before closing for discounted bento and prepared foods (look for discount stickers).
- Great for grabbing a hotel-room dinner or picking up edible souvenirs.

### Other Must-Try Foods
- **Gyudon** (beef bowl): Yoshinoya, Matsuya, Sukiya -- 400-600 yen for a filling meal
- **Curry rice:** CoCo Ichibanya -- customizable Japanese curry, ~700-1,000 yen
- **Convenience store food:** 7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart -- onigiri (150 yen), egg sandwiches, nikuman (meat buns), and surprisingly excellent prepared meals
- **Melon pan:** Crispy-sweet bread, found at bakeries everywhere (~200 yen)

## 5. Practical Tips

### Best Areas to Stay

| Area | Best For | Avg. Mid-Range Hotel | Pros | Cons |

| **Shinjuku** | First-timers, transport hub | $100-180/night | Best transport access (JR, Metro, buses, Odakyu for Hakone), nightlife (Golden Gai, Kabukicho), restaurants everywhere | Can feel overwhelming, busy station |

**Recommendation for this trip:** **Shinjuku** is the strongest pick. It sits on the Yamanote Line, has direct Odakyu access to Hakone, and is central to everything. Specifically, stay near the **east side** of Shinjuku Station for Golden Gai and Kabukicho access, or the **south side** for proximity to Fuunji and the Shinjuku Expressway Bus Terminal.

### Suica Card Setup
1. Download **Welcome Suica Mobile** app before departure (iOS/Android)
2. Set up in ~3 minutes, valid 180 days
3. Load via credit card in-app or cash at station machines
4. Tap in/out at all train gates, use for convenience store purchases
5. **Load recommendation:** Start with 3,000-5,000 yen, top up as needed

### September Weather in Tokyo
| | Early Sep | Mid Sep |
|--|----------|---------|
| High | 29-31C (84-88F) | 26-28C (79-82F) |
| Low | 22-24C (72-75F) | 20-22C (68-72F) |
| Rain | ~19 rainy days in the month | Same |
| Humidity | 73-82% | Gradually decreasing |

- **Still hot and humid.** Pack light, breathable clothing.
- **Typhoon season** -- 1-2 typhoons possible in September. Usually brief (half-day to two days of heavy rain). Rarely disrupts city plans significantly, but can affect Hakone day trip and outdoor plans.
- **Pack:** Compact umbrella (or buy a clear vinyl one at any convenience store for ~500 yen), light rain jacket, breathable shoes.
- Sunscreen and a small towel/handkerchief are essential.

### Tax-Free Shopping (Important: System Changes in Late 2026)
**During your September 2026 trip, the CURRENT system still applies:**
- Spend **5,000 yen or more** (before tax) at a single tax-free store in one visit
- Show your passport at checkout -> 10% consumption tax is deducted immediately
- Works at stores displaying the "Tax Free" logo (most major retailers, electronics stores, drug stores)
- Consumables (food, cosmetics) are sealed in a bag and should not be opened before leaving Japan

**Note:** Starting **November 1, 2026**, the system changes to a refund-at-customs model. But your trip is in September, so you'll enjoy the simpler current system.

**Best stores for tax-free:** Don Quijote (everything), Bic Camera, Yodobashi Camera, Matsumoto Kiyoshi (drugstore), Uniqlo.

## 7. Transport from Kyoto to Tokyo

### Shinkansen (Bullet Train)
| Train | Time | Frequency | Cost (One-Way) |
|-------|------|-----------|----------------|
| **Nozomi** (fastest) | ~2h15m | Every 10 min | 13,320 yen unreserved / ~14,170 yen reserved (~$89-95 USD) |
| **Hikari** | ~2h40m | Every 30 min | 13,320 yen unreserved / ~14,070 yen reserved |
| **Kodama** (all stops) | ~3h45m | Every 30 min | Same base fare |

### With a JR Pass
- **7-Day Ordinary JR Pass:** 50,000 yen (~$333 USD)
- The JR Pass covers **Hikari and Kodama** trains without surcharge
- For **Nozomi**, JR Pass holders must buy an additional "Nozomi/Mizuho Ticket" at **4,960 yen** per ride
- **Break-even analysis for JR Pass:** A Tokyo-Kyoto round trip on Hikari costs ~26,640-28,140 yen. The pass at 50,000 yen only makes sense if you're also doing other JR travel (e.g., Hakone/Odawara Shinkansen, airport NEX, etc.). For a single round trip Kyoto-Tokyo + Tokyo-only travel, buying individual tickets is cheaper.

### Without a JR Pass (Recommended for This Trip)
- **Buy individual Nozomi tickets:** ~14,170 yen one-way reserved seat (~$95 USD)
- Book via **SmartEX app** (English, works with foreign credit cards, pick up at station or use IC card)
- Reserve seats in advance, especially for weekend departures
- The Nozomi departs every ~10 minutes from Kyoto, so flexibility is high

### Recommendation
If this trip is **Tokyo-only with a Kyoto connection**, skip the JR Pass. Buy a one-way Nozomi ticket (~$95) and use Suica/Pasmo for all Tokyo transit. The math doesn't support a 7-day JR Pass ($333) unless you're also traveling to Osaka or other distant cities within the same week.
