## Airport to City

### Beijing Capital Airport (PEK) -- Recommended for city-center convenience
- **Location:** 32 km northeast of downtown
- **Airport Express Train:** PEK to Dongzhimen via Sanyuanqiao, 20-30 min, CNY 25 (~$3.50). Dongzhimen connects to subway Lines 2 and 13.
- **Taxi/Didi:** ~CNY 100-130 (~$14-18) to central Beijing, 45-70 min depending on traffic.
- **Subway:** Line connected at Terminal 3, very convenient.

### Beijing Daxing Airport (PKX) -- Newer, farther south
- **Location:** 50 km south of city center
- **Daxing Airport Express (subway):** 19 min to Caoqiao Station, CNY 25-35 (~$3.50-5). Transfer to Line 10 or Line 2.
- **High-speed train:** To Beijing West Railway Station in 29 min, CNY 30 (~$4). Runs 6:41am-8:56pm.
- **Taxi/Didi:** CNY 180-250 (~$25-35) to central Beijing.
- **Airport bus:** CNY 35-45, runs 5am-11pm.

**Recommendation:** Fly into PEK if possible -- it is closer to Dongcheng district and the major sights.

## Getting Around Beijing

### Subway (Metro)
- **Network:** 29 lines and growing; signs in Chinese AND English
- **Fare:** CNY 3-8 (~$0.40-1.10) per ride depending on distance
- **Hours:** ~5:00am to 11:00pm
- **Payment:** Tap international Visa/Mastercard directly at gates; or use WeChat Pay / Alipay QR code
- **Verdict:** Best option for most trips. Clean, fast, frequent, covers all major attractions.

### Didi (Ride-Hailing -- China's Uber)
- **English interface** available in the app
- **Cost:** Slightly cheaper than taxis; base fare ~CNY 13 for first 3 km
- **Best for:** Late-night rides (after subway closes), reaching places not near a metro station, groups of 2-3 splitting fares
- **Tip:** Download the Didi app before arriving in China and link payment.

### Taxis
- **Base fare:** CNY 13 (~$1.80) for first 3 km, then CNY 2.3/km (~$0.30)
- **After 11pm:** Slightly higher rates
- **Tip:** Show your destination in Chinese characters. Keep your hotel business card with the Chinese address.

### Bike Sharing
- **Apps:** Didi Bike, Hellobike, Meituan
- **Cost:** CNY 1.5-2 (~$0.20-0.30) per 30 min
- **Great for:** Short hops, hutong exploration

| **Time needed** | 3-5 hours on the wall | 2-4 hours on the wall | 4-6 hours on the wall |

### RECOMMENDATION: Mutianyu for First-Timers

Mutianyu is the best balance of authenticity, manageable crowds, and good facilities. It is the most popular section among foreign visitors for good reason. The toboggan ride down is a bonus highlight.

### Getting to Mutianyu -- DIY Options

**Option 1: Mubus (Tourist Bus) -- Easiest**
- Departs from Dongzhimen Metro Station at 9:00am and 10:00am daily
- Round-trip: CNY 80 (~$11) / One-way: CNY 40 (~$5.50)
- Book via WeChat account "mubus" or website www.beijingmubus.cn

**Option 2: Public Bus 916 + Transfer -- Cheapest**
- Take subway to Dongzhimen, then Bus 916 to Huairou (CNY 15, ~1 hr)
- Transfer to local bus or taxi to Mutianyu (CNY 25-50)
- Total: ~CNY 40-65 one-way

**Option 3: Bus 867 -- Direct (Seasonal)**
- Dongzhimen Wai Bus Station to Mutianyu, nonstop
- CNY 16 one-way (~$2.20), 2.5 hours, departs from 7am
- Only operates April to mid-November (you are in range for Sep!)

**Option 4: Didi / Private Car**
- CNY 300-500 round-trip (~$40-70) depending on wait time
- Most flexible; driver can wait at the parking area
- Best for groups of 2-4 splitting costs

**Option 5: Guided Tour**
- Half-day or full-day tours from ~CNY 300-600 per person ($40-80)
- Includes transport, guide, sometimes lunch
- Good if you don't want to deal with logistics

## Forbidden City -- Booking Strategy

### Key Facts
- **Visitor cap:** 40,000 per day (sells out frequently in September!)
- **No same-day online tickets** -- must book in advance
- **Ticket release:** 7 days in advance at 8:00 PM Beijing time
- **Peak season ticket (Apr 1 - Oct 31):** CNY 60 (~$8)
- **Low season ticket (Nov 1 - Mar 31):** CNY 40 (~$6)
- **Closed:** Every Monday (except national holidays)
- **Time needed:** 3-5 hours for a thorough visit
- **Hours:** 8:30am-5:00pm (last entry 4:00pm in peak season)

### Booking Strategy (CRITICAL -- Do This!)

1. **Set an alarm** for 8:00 PM Beijing time, exactly 7 days before your visit date (so Sep 8 at 8pm for a Sep 15 visit).
2. **Official website:** Book at https://gugong.ktmtech.cn/ (only official source).
3. **Bring your passport number** -- required during booking.
4. **Backup plan -- multi-platform approach:** If official site fails, immediately try Trip.com or Klook (authorized resellers) 1-2 minutes later.
5. **Emergency backup -- Donghuamen Gate:** Foreign passport holders can purchase same-day tickets at the Donghuamen Gate ticket office even when online shows "sold out." Arrive by 7:30am to queue.

### Visit Tips
- Enter through the **Meridian Gate (south entrance)** -- the only entry point.
- Exit through the **Gate of Divine Might (north exit)** -- one-way flow.
- Audio guide available: CNY 40 (English). Worth it for context.
- Visit the **Treasure Gallery** (CNY 10 extra) and **Clock Gallery** (CNY 10 extra) -- both excellent.
- Morning visits recommended -- less crowded before 10am.

## Other Must-See Attractions

### Tiananmen Square
- **Cost:** Free
- **Time needed:** 30-60 min
- **Note:** Security screening required to enter. Bring passport.
- **Combine with:** Forbidden City (it is right next to it -- visit the square first, then walk north into the Forbidden City).

### Temple of Heaven
- **Entrance fee:** CNY 45 (peak season) / CNY 40 (off-season)
- **Time needed:** 2-3 hours
- **Highlights:** Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests (iconic circular building), Echo Wall, Imperial Vault of Heaven
- **Tip:** Go early morning (6-8am) to see locals doing tai chi, dancing, and singing -- an unforgettable cultural experience. The park opens at 6am even though the buildings open later (8am).
- **Nearest metro:** Tiantandongmen Station (Line 5)

### Summer Palace
- **Entrance fee:** CNY 30 (grounds only) / CNY 60 (combo with gardens)
- **Time needed:** 3-4 hours
- **Highlights:** Kunming Lake, Longevity Hill, Long Corridor (728m of painted scenes), Marble Boat
- **Tip:** Take a dragon boat across Kunming Lake. Go on a weekday if possible.
- **Nearest metro:** Beigongmen Station (Line 4)

### Hutong Tours (Rickshaw Ride)
- **Cost:** CNY 100-200 (~$14-28) depending on duration
- **Duration:** 30-90 min
- **Best areas:** Shichahai (near Houhai Lake), Nanluoguxiang
- **What you will see:** Traditional courtyard houses (siheyuan), local life, narrow alleyways
- **Tip:** Negotiate the price before getting in. Or just walk through the hutongs for free.

### 798 Art District (Dashanzi)
- **Cost:** Free to enter the district; individual galleries may charge
- **Time needed:** 2-3 hours
- **What it is:** Former industrial factory complex converted into Beijing's contemporary art hub -- galleries, cafes, street art, studios
- **Best for:** Afternoon/evening stroll
- **Nearest metro:** Wangjing South (Line 14/15), then taxi or bus

## Food Guide

### Peking Duck -- The Must-Have Meal

| Restaurant | Style | Cost (whole duck) | Vibe | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Da Dong** | Modern, lighter/less fat | CNY 398 (~$55) | Upscale, sleek | Best "modern" duck. Crispy translucent skin. Smaller portions. |
| **Quanjude** (Hepingmen branch) | Traditional since 1864 | CNY 200-300 (~$28-42) | Historic institution | Iconic but quality varies by branch. ONLY go to Hepingmen. |
| **Siji Minfu** | Traditional, great value | CNY 198-268 (~$27-37) | Popular, refined | Best value-to-quality ratio. The locals' favorite. |
| **Duck de Chine** | Franco-Chinese | CNY 388 (~$53) | Courtyard setting | Larger portions than Da Dong at similar price. Beautiful ambiance. |
| **Bianyifang** | Oldest (est. 1416!) | CNY 180-260 (~$25-36) | Historic | Closed-oven method -- less crispy skin but juicier meat. |
| **Cheap alternative** | Any local duck restaurant | CNY 80-150 (~$11-21) | Casual | Ask your hotel for a neighborhood recommendation. |

**Recommendation:** Siji Minfu for best bang-for-buck. Da Dong or Duck de Chine for a splurge.

### Other Must-Try Foods

| Food | What It Is | Where to Find | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Jianbing** | Savory crepe with egg, crispy wonton, scallions, sauces | Street vendors everywhere, especially mornings | CNY 8-15 (~$1-2) |
| **Lamb skewers (yangrou chuan)** | Cumin-spiced grilled lamb on sticks | Night markets, Wangfujing, Muslim Quarter, Guijie Street | CNY 5-10 per skewer |
| **Hot pot** | DIY boiling pot with meats, veggies, dipping sauces | Haidilao (chain, great service), Donglaishun (traditional Beijing-style) | CNY 80-150/person (~$11-21) |
| **Zhajiangmian** | Beijing-style noodles with soybean paste sauce | Local noodle shops throughout the city | CNY 15-30 (~$2-4) |
| **Tanghulu** | Candied fruit on a stick (hawthorn berries) | Street vendors | CNY 10-15 (~$1.50-2) |
| **Luzhu huoshao** | Stewed offal with bread (local Beijing soul food) | Traditional restaurants in Qianmen or hutong areas | CNY 20-40 (~$3-6) |

### Wangfujing Snack Street
- **Hours:** Opens 24 hours; night market atmosphere best 5pm-10pm
- **What to expect:** Snacks from all over China -- stinky tofu, candied hawthorn, mutton skewers, coconut treats, steamed cakes, and yes, the infamous scorpion-on-a-stick (mostly a tourist gimmick)
- **Note:** As of 2025, parts of the street are under renovation with food courts moving to basement areas. Check current status before visiting.
- **Tip:** Visit on a weekday evening for manageable crowds.

### Guijie (Ghost Street) -- Alternative Food Street
- Open until late at night; less touristy than Wangfujing
- Famous for crayfish and spicy hotpot
- Location: Dongzhimen area (walking distance from Dongzhimen metro)

## Practical Tips

### VPN -- ESSENTIAL (Set Up BEFORE Arrival)
- Google, Gmail, Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp, Twitter/X are ALL blocked in China.
- **Download and install a VPN before you leave.** Popular options: ExpressVPN, Astrill, NordVPN.
- Test it before departure.
- Without a VPN you cannot access most Western services.

### Mobile Payments (WeChat Pay / Alipay)
- China is essentially cashless. Even street vendors prefer mobile payment.
- **Alipay** is recommended for short-term tourists -- easier setup, better for larger establishments.
- **WeChat Pay** is more common at small vendors and street stalls.
- Both now accept international Visa/Mastercard linking.
- **Set up both apps and link your card before arriving.**
- Still carry some cash (CNY 500-1000) as backup for small vendors or emergencies.

### Maps and Navigation
- Google Maps does not work well in China.
- **Download Amap (Gaode Maps)** -- most accurate and detailed for China, with English support.
- Apple Maps works reasonably well as a secondary option.
- Download offline maps as a fallback.

### Weather in Mid-September
- **Temperature:** Highs 26-30C (79-86F); lows 16-20C (61-68F)
- **Conditions:** Mostly clear, dry, comfortable. Occasional light rain.
- **Rainfall:** ~46mm across ~8 rainy days for the whole month
- **What to pack:** Light layers, a light jacket for evenings, comfortable walking shoes, sunscreen, light rain jacket just in case.

### Air Quality in September
- **September is the best month for air quality in Beijing.**
- Average AQI: 56 (moderate). Lowest: 26 (good). Highest: 130 (sensitive groups).
- No unhealthy days recorded in September. Very favorable for outdoor sightseeing.
- You will likely have clear skies -- great for Great Wall photos.

### Visa Requirements
- **240-hour visa-free transit** is available for US (and many other) passport holders through the end of 2026.
- Requirements: Valid passport (3+ months remaining), confirmed onward ticket to a THIRD country (not the same country you arrived from), stay within permitted areas.
- **If coming from Tokyo and continuing to another country (e.g., not back to Japan immediately):** You qualify for 240-hour (10-day) visa-free transit.
- **If your trip is Tokyo > Beijing > Tokyo:** You may NOT qualify for transit-without-visa since you are returning to the same country. In this case, apply for a tourist visa (L visa) in advance. Check the latest rules carefully.
- **Important:** Verify your specific nationality's eligibility and current policy before traveling. Rules can change.

### Language
- English is limited outside tourist areas and international hotels.
- **Download a translation app** (Google Translate with offline Chinese pack, or Pleco for dictionary).
- Hotel staff at mid-range and above hotels generally speak basic English.
- Subway signage is bilingual (Chinese/English).

### Electricity
- China uses Type A, C, and I plugs. Voltage: 220V.
- Bring a universal adapter if coming from Japan (Japan uses 100V Type A).

## Where to Stay

### Recommended Area: Dongcheng District
- **Why:** Heart of old Beijing. Walking distance to Forbidden City, Tiananmen Square, Wangfujing, and many hutongs. Excellent subway connections.
- **Sub-areas:**
  - **Wangfujing:** Shopping, dining, nightlife. Most convenient for first-timers.
  - **Near Forbidden City / Qianmen:** Historic atmosphere, close to major sights.
  - **Nanluoguxiang / Drum Tower area:** Hipster hutong vibe, cafes, bars, boutiques.

## Suggested 3-Night Itinerary Outline

### Day 1 (Sep 15) -- Arrive + Central Beijing
- Arrive Beijing, transfer to hotel (Dongcheng)
- Afternoon: Tiananmen Square + Forbidden City (book tickets 7 days prior -- Sep 8 at 8pm Beijing time!)
- Evening: Peking Duck dinner (Siji Minfu or Da Dong)
- After dinner: Stroll Wangfujing Snack Street

### Day 2 (Sep 16) -- Great Wall Day
- Early departure (8-9am) to Mutianyu Great Wall via Mubus or Didi
- Spend 3-5 hours hiking the wall
- Toboggan ride down
- Return to Beijing by late afternoon
- Evening: Hutong rickshaw tour around Shichahai / Houhai Lake area
- Dinner: Lamb skewers and cold beer at a hutong restaurant

### Day 3 (Sep 17) -- Temples, Palace, and Culture
- Morning: Temple of Heaven (arrive early for tai chi scene)
- Midday: Lunch near Qianmen
- Afternoon: Summer Palace (boat ride on Kunming Lake)
- Late afternoon/evening: 798 Art District OR more hutong exploration in Nanluoguxiang
- Farewell dinner: Hot pot at Haidilao or traditional option

### Day 4 (Sep 18) -- Departure
- Last-minute shopping or sightseeing
- Transfer to airport
