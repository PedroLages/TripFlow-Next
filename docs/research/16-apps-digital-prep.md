## VPN for China

### What Gets Blocked in China

- **Google:** Search, Gmail, Maps, YouTube, Drive
- **Social:** Instagram, Facebook, Twitter/X, WhatsApp, Telegram, TikTok
- **News:** Most Western news sites (NYTimes, BBC, etc.)
- **What works:** WeChat, Alipay, Apple Maps, Bing, Baidu Maps

### Recommended VPNs

| VPN | Monthly Cost | Speed | Reliability in China | Setup Ease |
|-----|--------------|-------|---------------------|------------|
| **ExpressVPN** | ~$8 | Fast | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Easy |
| **Astrill VPN** | ~$15 | Very Fast | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Easy |
| **NordVPN** | ~$5 | Fast | ⭐⭐⭐ | Easy |
| **Surfshark** | ~$2-3 | Medium | ⭐⭐⭐ | Easy |

**Recommendation:** ExpressVPN for most travelers. Astrill if budget allows (most reliable in China).

### VPN Setup Steps

1. **Subscribe BEFORE leaving home** — cannot sign up from China
2. **Download app** from App Store / Play Store BEFORE arriving
3. **Install and test** while still at home
4. **Screenshot setup** in case you need to reinstall
5. **Enable auto-connect** on untrusted networks

### eSIM with Built-in VPN (Simplest Option)

Some eSIM providers include built-in proxy/VPN that routes through overseas servers:

- **Airalo China eSIM:** Check if "global routing" is included
- **Holafly:** Some plans include VPN routing
- **Benefit:** No separate VPN app needed

**Limitation:** Still won't work for banking apps that detect location via IP.

### Overview

| Country | Primary Apps | Cash Needed? | Foreign Cards Work? |
|---------|--------------|--------------|---------------------|
| **China** | Alipay, WeChat Pay | Rarely | Yes, with setup |
| **Hong Kong** | Octopus card, credit cards | No | Yes, widely |
| **Japan** | IC cards (Suica/ICOCA), credit cards | Minimal | Yes, widely |

---

### Alipay Setup (Do This First!)

**Why:** Alipay has better foreign card support than WeChat Pay.

1. **Download Alipay** from App Store / Play Store
2. **Open app** → Tap "Sign Up" → Select "International Account"
3. **Enter phone number** (use your regular number)
4. **Enter SMS verification code**
5. **Create password** → Login
6. **Complete Real-Name Verification** (CRITICAL — takes up to 3 days):
   - Profile → Real-Name Verification
   - Upload photo of passport
   - Wait for approval (usually 1-3 days)
7. **Link payment method:**
   - Me → Cards → Add Card
   - Add Visa, Mastercard, AMEX, or Discover
   - Card must support international transactions

**Alipay TourCard** (Optional but useful):
- Special rate for foreign visitors
- Better exchange rates than regular Alipay
- Search "TourCard" in Alipay app after setup

### WeChat Pay Setup

**Why:** Some vendors only accept WeChat Pay. Good backup.

1. **Download WeChat** from App Store / Play Store
2. **Sign up** with phone number
3. **Complete profile:**
   - Me → Settings → My Profile
   - Add name, region
4. **Enable WeChat Pay:**
   - Me → Services → Pay → Wallet
   - Verify with passport
   - Link international credit card
5. **Wait for approval** (1-3 days)

### IC Card Setup (Japan)

**Welcome Suica Mobile** (iPhone — Recommended):
1. Open Apple Wallet on iPhone
2. Tap "+" → Search "Suica" → "Welcome Suica"
3. Enter credit card details
4. Add ¥1,000-5,000 starting balance
5. Done! Tap to ride trains, pay at stores

**Physical ICOCA** (Android or iPhone):
1. Buy at JR ticket machine at KIX airport
2. Cost: ¥2,000 (¥500 deposit + ¥1,500 balance)
3. Top up at any station machine or convenience store
4. Tap in/out at train gates

**Important:** ICOCA can only be refunded in JR West areas. Use Welcome Suica if continuing to Tokyo (can't refund ICOCA there).

### Octopus Card Setup (Hong Kong)

1. **Buy at airport** (MTR Customer Service Center after customs)
2. **Tourist Octopus:** HK$39 (no deposit, keeps value)
3. **Standard Octopus:** HK$150 (HK$50 deposit + HK$100 value)
4. **Top up** at any MTR station, 7-Eleven, or Circle K
5. **Refund** before departure: HK$50 deposit + remaining balance

**Contactless alternative:** Blue gates at some MTR stations accept Visa/Mastercard directly. But buses/trams do NOT — get Octopus card.

## Navigation & Maps

### China: Use Amap (Gaode Maps)

**Why:** Google Maps is blocked or severely limited in China.

| Feature | Works? |
|---------|--------|
| Google Maps | Partial — no transit directions |
| **Amap (Gaode)** | ✅ Full functionality |
| Apple Maps | ✅ Good alternative |

**Amap Tips:**
- Download English version if available
- Search works with English names for major attractions
- Pin hotel location before relying on it
- Download offline map of Shanghai + Beijing

### Japan: Google Maps + Navitime

**Google Maps excels in Japan:**
- Comprehensive transit directions
- Real-time train delays
- Timetables for every station
- English search works perfectly

**Navitime backup:**
- More detailedJR Pass info
- Platform numbers for transfers
- Seating car indicators

### Hong Kong: Citymapper + Google Maps

**Citymapper covers HK well:**
- MTR routes with fares
- Bus routes with real-time
- Octopus card balance tracking
