## 1. Getting Around Shanghai

### Metro System
- **Scale**: 21 lines, 517+ stations -- one of the world's largest metro networks
- **Hours**: First trains ~5:30-6:00 AM; last trains ~10:30-11:30 PM; frequency every 2-6 minutes during the day
- **Fares**: 3-15 RMB per trip (~$0.40-$2 USD) depending on distance
- **Tickets**: Single-journey tokens from vending machines (English interface available), or scan QR codes via the Metro Daduhui app or Alipay mini-program
- **Important rule**: Your entry and exit methods must match -- if you scan a QR code to enter, you must use the same QR code to exit (not a physical ticket)
- **Navigation**: All stations have bilingual Chinese/English signage. Download Gaode Maps (Amap) for route planning. 5G and free WiFi available throughout the network

### Taxi / Ride-Hailing (Didi)
- **Didi Chuxing** is China's equivalent of Uber. Download "DiDi - Ride Hailing in China" before you travel
- **Setup**: Works with international phone numbers (needs SMS verification). Supports English interface
- **Payment**: Accepts Visa, Mastercard, JCB, Diners Club directly, plus WeChat Pay, Alipay, and Apple Pay
- **Ride tiers**: Express (cheapest, like UberX), Premier (nicer car), Luxe (premium)
- **Alternative access**: Use the Didi mini-program within Alipay if you have trouble with the standalone app
- **Tip**: Have the last 4 digits of your phone number written down -- drivers sometimes ask for verification in Chinese
- **Communication**: In-app translation feature helps bridge the language gap

### Airport Transport

## 2. Must-See Attractions

### The Bund (Waitan)
- **What**: Shanghai's iconic 1.5 km waterfront promenade along the Huangpu River, featuring 52 buildings in various Western architectural styles (Art Deco, Baroque, Gothic, etc.) on one side and the futuristic Pudong skyline across the river
- **Best time of day**: Visit twice if possible -- daytime for architecture and river breeze, then return around **6:00 PM for the light show** when the Pudong skyline illuminates
- **Cost**: Free to walk the promenade
- **Getting there**: Metro Line 2 or 10 to Nanjing East Road Station, or Line 2 to Lujiazui for the Pudong side
- **Late August tip**: Evenings will be warm and humid but the river breeze helps. Go after 5:30 PM to avoid the worst of the daytime heat

### Zhujiajiao Water Town
- **What**: A 1,700-year-old ancient water town with canals, stone bridges, and traditional architecture -- often called "the Venice of Shanghai"
- **Distance**: ~48 km west of central Shanghai, within Shanghai municipality
- **How to get there**:
  - **Metro Line 17** to Zhujiajiao Station, then 15-minute walk to the old town (recommended, ~1-1.5 hours from central Shanghai)
  - **Taxi / Didi**: ~100-150 RMB one-way from central Shanghai, ~45-60 min depending on traffic
  - **Tourist bus** from People's Square (daily departures)
- **Entrance fees**:
  - Walking around the town: **FREE**
  - Combined ticket for 8 attractions: ~60 RMB (~$8)
  - Combined ticket for 4 attractions: ~30-40 RMB (~$4-5)
  - Gondola boat ride: ~80 RMB (~$11) per boat
- **Hours**: 8:30 AM - 5:00 PM (some shops stay open later)
- **Tips**: Go on a **weekday** to avoid crowds. Aug 27-30 are Wed-Sat, so aim for Wed or Thu. Budget half a day (3-4 hours on site)

### 1000 Trees (Tian'an Qianshu)
- **What**: A spectacular mixed-use building designed by Thomas Heatherwick (Heatherwick Studio), featuring 1,000 structural columns each topped with a tree/planter. Inspired by China's Yellow Mountain, it resembles a "floating forest" or modern Hanging Gardens of Babylon
- **Location**: 600 Moganshan Road, along the Suzhou River (technically in Putuo District, near the Jing'an border)
- **Getting there**: Metro Line 13 to Jiangning Road Station, Exit 1, then ~8-minute walk
- **Cost**: Free to visit the exterior and public areas. The building houses cafes, art galleries, local designer shops, and the Heatherwick Studio exhibition space (also free)
- **Best time**: **Sunset** is ideal -- the lighting on the building is stunning. Nighttime is also excellent when the "mountain" silhouette reflects in the Suzhou River
- **Don't miss**: The South Facade art wall (tribute to local graffiti artists), rooftop terrace views, and the Heatherwick Studio space with models of their global projects

## 3. Food

### Must-Try Shanghai Dishes

| Dish | Description | Where to Try |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Xiaolongbao** (soup dumplings) | Thin-skinned steamed dumplings filled with pork and hot soup. Originated in nearby Jiangsu Province | Jia Jia Tang Bao (Huanghe Road), Din Tai Fung, Lai Lai (Michelin Bib Gourmand), Fuchun (est. 1885) |
| **Shengjianbao** (pan-fried buns) | Crispy golden-brown bottom, soft steamed top, filled with pork and soupy broth | Yang's Fried Dumplings (multiple branches), Da Hu Chun (est. 1932) |
| **Hongshao Rou** (red-braised pork) | Sweet, rich braised pork belly in soy sauce and sugar -- a Shanghainese staple | Local Shanghainese restaurants |
| **Cong You Bing** (scallion pancakes) | Crispy, flaky pan-fried flatbread with scallions | Street vendors, breakfast shops |
| **Xiao Wonton** | Delicate thin-skinned wontons in clear broth | Da Hu Chun, local noodle shops |
| **Hairy Crab** | Iconic Shanghai delicacy with rich roe | **NOT in season in late August** -- season starts late September/October. Skip for this trip |

### Hairy Crab Note
The prized hairy crab season runs from late September through November (9th and 10th lunar months). In late August the crabs have not yet matured, so it is not worth seeking them out on this trip. If you return in October-November, top spots include Cheng Long Hang (Michelin one-star, est. 2002), Wang Bao He (est. 1744), and Cejerdary (famous crab noodles using roe from 12 crabs per bowl).

### Key Food Areas

**Yuyuan Garden / City God Temple Bazaar**
- Nanxiang Steamed Bun Restaurant -- the most famous xiaolongbao spot in the old town area
- Street food galore: scallion pancakes, stinky tofu, tanghulu (candied fruit), fried skewers
- Touristy but atmospheric; expect lines at popular stalls

**Nanjing Road**
- China's longest pedestrian shopping street with hundreds of restaurants
- Shanghai First Foodhall -- large food court with diverse local and regional Chinese cuisines
- Godly Vegetarian Restaurant (445 Nanjing West Road) for vegetarian mock duck

**French Concession**
- Best area for cafe culture, brunch spots, and upscale dining
- Lost Heaven (38 Gaoyou Road) -- excellent Yunnan folk cuisine with vegetarian options
- Numerous independent coffee shops and bakeries along tree-lined streets
- More international dining options (Japanese, Italian, French)

**Huanghe Road Food Street**
- Near People's Square, walkable to the Bund
- Jia Jia Tang Bao -- legendary no-frills xiaolongbao (expect shared tables, long queues)

## 4. Practical Tips

### SIM / eSIM
- **Recommended**: Buy an eSIM before departure for instant connectivity on landing
- **Providers**: Airalo, Holafly, Flexiroam, Roamify -- all offer China data eSIMs
- **Key advantage**: Some China eSIMs include built-in VPN/proxy that routes through overseas servers, letting you access Google, WhatsApp, etc. without a separate VPN. Check this when purchasing -- it is a major convenience factor
- **Physical SIM**: Available at airport counters upon arrival if your phone does not support eSIM

### VPN Needs (Critical)
- **Blocked in China**: Google (Search, Maps, Gmail, YouTube), WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, Twitter/X, Telegram, most Western news sites
- **What works natively**: WeChat, Alipay, Apple Maps, Bing, Baidu Maps
- **Solution**: Install a VPN **before arriving in China**. VPN apps cannot be downloaded from app stores while in China
- **Recommended VPNs**: ExpressVPN (~$8/month), Astrill VPN (~$15/month). Download, install, and test before your flight
- **Alternative**: Get an eSIM with built-in VPN/proxy (simplest option)

### WeChat Pay & Alipay Setup (Do This Before You Travel)
- **Both apps** now allow foreigners to link international Visa, Mastercard, AMEX, or Discover cards directly -- no Chinese bank account needed
- **Setup steps**:
  1. Download both Alipay and WeChat before departure
  2. Register with your international phone number
  3. Complete **Real-Name Verification** using your passport (takes up to 3 days -- do this early)
  4. Link your international credit/debit card
- **Alipay** tends to have fewer transaction hassles with foreign cards
- **Why both**: Some vendors only accept one or the other. Having both is your backup plan
- **Usage**: QR code payments are used everywhere -- restaurants, convenience stores, metro, taxis, even street food vendors. Cash is rarely used in Shanghai but carry some small bills as backup

### Best Areas to Stay

| Area | Vibe | Price Range | Best For |
|------|------|-------------|----------|
| **French Concession** | Tree-lined streets, cafes, quieter at night, expat-friendly | Mid to high | First-time visitors, food lovers, walkability |
| **Jing'an** | Trendy, central, good metro access, slightly lower prices | Mid-range | Budget-conscious travelers who want a central location |
| **The Bund / Huangpu** | Iconic views, upscale, 24/7 traffic noise | High | Luxury travelers, Bund proximity |
| **Lujiazui (Pudong)** | Modern skyscrapers, business district, river views | Mid to high | Skyline views, Shanghai Tower access |

**Recommendation for this trip**: The **French Concession** or **Jing'an** offer the best balance of walkability, food options, metro access, and atmosphere. Both put you within 15-20 minutes of the Bund by metro and close to 1000 Trees.

### Weather in Late August
- **Temperature**: Highs around 33-35C (91-95F), can spike to 38C (100F). Lows around 25-27C (77-81F)
- **Humidity**: 82-84% average -- it will feel oppressive
- **Rain**: Expect thunderstorms; roughly 8-15 rainy days in August. Carry a compact umbrella daily
- **Typhoon risk**: August is peak typhoon season. Monitor forecasts before and during your trip
- **What to pack**:
  - Lightweight, breathable clothing (cotton or moisture-wicking fabrics)
  - Comfortable walking shoes that can handle wet conditions
  - Compact umbrella or rain jacket
  - Sunscreen and sunglasses
  - Portable fan or cooling towel
  - Refillable water bottle (stay hydrated)
- **Strategy**: Plan outdoor sightseeing for early morning (before 10 AM) or evening (after 5 PM). Use midday for indoor attractions, malls, or air-conditioned restaurants

## 6. Day Trip Logistics: Zhujiajiao Water Town

### Getting There

**Option A: Metro Line 17 (Recommended)**
- Take Metro Line 17 from central Shanghai to **Zhujiajiao Station** (the terminus)
- You will likely need to transfer at **Hongqiao Railway Station** or **Xujing** depending on your starting point
- Total journey: **~60-90 minutes** from central Shanghai
- Cost: **~7-10 RMB** (~$1-1.50) one way
- From Zhujiajiao Station: 15-minute walk south to the ancient town entrance, or take a short bus/taxi

**Option B: Taxi / Didi**
- Duration: **~45-60 minutes** from central Shanghai (can be longer in rush hour)
- Cost: **~100-150 RMB** (~$14-21) one way
- Advantage: Door-to-door, more comfortable in the August heat
- Disadvantage: Significantly more expensive; could be hard to hail a return ride from the town

**Option C: Tourist Bus**
- Departs from **People's Square** (Shanghai Sightseeing Bus Center)
- Less frequent, less flexible schedule

### Recommended Itinerary for Zhujiajiao
- **Depart**: 8:00-9:00 AM from your hotel (beat the heat and crowds)
- **Arrive**: ~9:30-10:30 AM
- **Explore**: Walk the canal-side streets, cross the Fangsheng Bridge (five-arch stone bridge from 1571), visit Kezhi Garden, browse the alleys for local snacks (zongzi rice dumplings are a specialty)
- **Boat ride**: Optional 20-30 minute gondola ride along the canals (~80 RMB)
- **Lunch**: Eat at a canal-side restaurant -- try the local specialties (braised pork knuckle, zongzi)
- **Return**: Aim to head back by 1:00-2:00 PM to avoid the worst afternoon heat
- **Total time on-site**: 3-4 hours is sufficient

### Suggested 3-Night Schedule Framework

| Day | Date | Focus | Notes |
|-----|------|-------|-------|
| **Day 1** | Wed Aug 27 | Arrival + The Bund evening | Settle in, explore nearby area, walk the Bund at sunset/night for the light show (~6 PM) |
| **Day 2** | Thu Aug 28 | Zhujiajiao day trip + Pudong evening | Early departure to water town, return by early afternoon, Shanghai Tower or Oriental Pearl at night |
| **Day 3** | Fri Aug 29 | 1000 Trees + City exploration | Morning at 1000 Trees (sunset is ideal but schedule flexible), Yu Garden + Yuyuan Bazaar, Tianzifang, French Concession wandering, Nanjing Road |
| **Day 4** | Sat Aug 30 | Final morning + Departure | Last-minute shopping or revisit a favorite spot, depart |
