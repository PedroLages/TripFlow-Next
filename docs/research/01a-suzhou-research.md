## Overview

**Suzhou** (苏州) is a 2,500-year-old city located ~80 km west of Shanghai, often called "Venice of the East" for its canals and classical gardens. A UNESCO World Heritage site for its nine classical gardens, Suzhou is renowned for silk production, traditional architecture, and refined water-town culture.

**Zhouzhuang** (周庄) is a 900-year-old water town about 30 km southeast of Suzhou, famous for its well-preserved Ming/Qing dynasty architecture, stone bridges, and canal views.

**Best time to visit**: September is ideal -- warm but not scorching (25-30°C), fewer crowds than summer, gardens still lush.

## 1. Getting There & Back

### Shanghai to Suzhou

**High-Speed Train (Recommended)**
- **Stations**: Shanghai Railway Station (main) → Suzhou Railway Station
- **Duration**: 25-35 minutes
- **Frequency**: Every 10-20 minutes, 6:00 AM - 10:00 PM
- **Cost**: ~40-50 RMB one-way (2nd class), ~65 RMB (1st class)
- **Booking**: 12306 app (Chinese interface) or Trip.com (English, small fee)
- **Tip**: Book morning departure to maximize day

**Alternative: Bus**
- **Departure**: Shanghai Long-Distance Bus Station
- **Duration**: ~1.5-2 hours
- **Cost**: ~35-50 RMB
- **Not recommended**: Slower than train, less frequent

### Suzhou to Zhouzhuang

**Bus + Taxi Combination**
- **Suzhou North Bus Station** → Zhouzhuang
- **Duration**: ~1 hour by bus + taxi
- **Cost**: ~20-30 RMB total
- **Frequency**: Buses every 30-60 minutes

**Direct Taxi/Rideshare (Recommended)**
- **Duration**: ~30-40 minutes
- **Cost**: ~80-120 RMB one-way
- **Didi**: Works in Suzhou, accept international cards
- **Tip**: Share ride with other travelers if possible at Zhouzhuang entrance

### Zhouzhuang to Shanghai

**Option 1: Return via Suzhou**
- Zhouzhuang → Suzhou by taxi (~80 RMB)
- Suzhou → Shanghai by train (~50 RMB)
- Total: ~130 RMB, ~2 hours

**Option 2: Direct bus to Shanghai**
- Zhouzhuang Bus Station → Shanghai
- **Duration**: ~1.5-2 hours
- **Cost**: ~40-50 RMB
- **Frequency**: Less frequent, check schedule

## 2. Suzhou Classical Gardens

Suzhou's gardens are masterpieces of Chinese landscape design, combining rocks, water, plants, and architecture in harmonious compositions.

### Humble Administrator's Garden (Zhuozheng Yuan)

**What**: Largest and most famous of Suzhou's gardens, UNESCO World Heritage

**Highlights**:
- Central pond with pavilions
- Lotus flowers in summer (late August)
- Zigzag bridges and covered walkways
- Museum of garden bonsai and artifacts

**Practical Info**:
- **Cost**: 80 RMB in summer (Apr-Oct), 50 RMB in winter
- **Hours**: 7:30 AM - 5:30 PM (last entry 5:00 PM)
- **Time needed**: 1.5-2 hours
- **Best time**: Early morning (before tour groups) or late afternoon
- **Tip**: Rent audio guide (20 RMB) or download English commentary

**Address**: 178 Northeast Street, Gusu District

### Liu Garden (Liu Yuan)

**What**: Exquisite smaller garden, masterful use of space

**Highlights**:
- Famous "Four Seasons" rockery
- Circular moon gate views
- Intimate scale, fewer crowds
- Traditional opera stage

**Practical Info**:
- **Cost**: 55 RMB (Apr-Oct), 40 RMB (Nov-Mar)
- **Hours**: 7:30 AM - 5:30 PM
- **Time needed**: 45-60 minutes
- **Best for**: Photography, architecture enthusiasts

**Address**: 338 Liuyuan Road, Gusu District

### Other Notable Gardens (If Time Permits)

| Garden | Specialty | Cost | Time Needed |
|--------|-----------|------|-------------|
| **Master of Nets Garden** | Smallest but most exquisite, UNESCO | 40 RMB | 30-45 min |
| **Couple's Retreat Garden** | Dual design, intimate | 50 RMB | 30-45 min |
| **Lion Grove Garden** | Rockery maze, fun to explore | 40 RMB | 30-45 min |
| **Hanshan Temple** | Famous poem, historic temple | 20 RMB | 30 min |

## 3. Zhouzhuang Water Town

**What**: Best-preserved water town in the region, 900 years of history

**Must-See**:
- **Double Bridges** (Shide Bridge and Yongan Bridge) - iconic photo spot
- **Shen Hall** - grand mansion of wealthy merchant
- **Zhang Hall** - Ming dynasty residence over water
- **Folk Customs Museum** - local history and culture

**Canal Boat Ride**:
- **Cost**: 180-200 RMB per boat (seats 4-6 people, share with others)
- **Duration**: ~20 minutes
- **Route**: Main canals, under stone bridges
- **Tip**: Go at sunset for golden light on ancient houses

**Practical Info**:
- **Entrance fee**: 100 RMB (includes most attractions)
- **Hours**: 8:00 AM - 9:00 PM (shops open later)
- **Time needed**: 2-3 hours
- **Avoid**: Weekends and holidays (extremely crowded)
- **Best**: Late afternoon for atmosphere, early morning for photos

## 4. Historic Streets & Areas

### Pingjiang Road (Pingjiang Lu)

**What**: Historic canal street with traditional architecture, cafes, boutiques

**Highlights**:
- Stone bridges over canal
- Traditional tea houses
- Local snacks and crafts
- Evening lantern displays

**Cost**: Free to stroll
**Time**: 45-60 minutes
**Best**: Late afternoon into evening

### Shantang Street (Seven Mile Shantang)

**What**: Popular tourist street along canal, busier but more extensive than Pingjiang

**Highlights**:
- Night boat rides
- Street food vendors
- Traditional architecture
- More souvenir shops

**Cost**: Free to stroll
**Time**: 1-2 hours
**Best**: Evening for lights and atmosphere

## 6. Shopping

### Silk Products

Suzhou is China's silk capital -- quality and prices better than Shanghai.

**Where to Buy**:
- **Suzhou No. 1 Silk Mill** - reputable, slightly higher prices
- **Pingjiang Road shops** - variety, negotiate
- **Zhouzhuang** - silk embroidery, unique designs

**What to Buy**:
- Silk scarves ($10-50)
- Silk pajamas/robes ($30-100)
- Silk embroidery art ($20-200)
- Silk pillowcases ($15-40)

### Other Souvenirs

- Traditional fans (paper or silk)
- Tea (scented teas, biluochun green tea)
- Calligraphy supplies
- Snacks (green tea cookies, pastries)

## 7. Practical Tips

### Weather (Late August)

- **Temperature**: 25-32°C / 77-90°F
- **Humidity**: High
- **Rain**: Afternoon thunderstorms possible
- **What to wear**: Light, breathable clothing, comfortable shoes, umbrella, sunscreen

### Payment & Apps

- **Alipay/WeChat Pay**: Widely accepted, set up before visit
- **Cash**: Bring 200-300 RMB for small vendors and backups
- **Apps**: Didi for taxis, 12306 or Trip.com for trains
- **Language**: Limited English, download Google Translate Chinese pack

### Luggage Storage

- **Suzhou Railway Station**: Lockers available (3-5 RMB)
- **Gardens**: No storage facilities, travel light
- **Tip**: Store at station, explore city, retrieve before Zhouzhuang

### Photography Tips

- **Best light**: Early morning (7-9 AM) in gardens, late afternoon (4-6 PM) in Zhouzhuang
- **Crowds**: Arrive at gardens when they open (7:30 AM)
- **Reflections**: Canals offer great mirror shots on calm days
- **Architecture**: Focus on rooflines, bridges, and garden details

### Avoiding Tourist Traps

- **Skip**: Overpriced silk tours pushed by touts
- **Skip**: Expensive "package deals" for gardens
- **Do**: Buy individual garden tickets instead
- **Do**: Eat at local noodle shops instead of tourist restaurants
