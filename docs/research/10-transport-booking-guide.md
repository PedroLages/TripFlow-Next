## Route 1: Shanghai → Hong Kong

### Decision Matrix

| Factor | Flight | High-Speed Train (G99) | Overnight Sleeper (G899) |
|--------|--------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Duration** | 2h 40m | ~8 hours | ~11 hours (overnight) |
| **Cost** | $60-250 | $124-397 | $100-140 |
| **Convenience** | Airport transfers needed | Arrives West Kowloon (central) | Arrives West Kowloon + saves hotel night |
| **Best For** | Time-saving | Experience / City center | Budget / Adventure |
| **Booking** | Google Flights | 12306 app (15 days before) | 12306 app (15 days before) |

### Flight Option

**Airlines:**
- Full-service: Cathay Pacific, China Eastern, Hong Kong Airlines
- Budget: Spring Airlines, Juneyao Airlines, Shanghai Airlines

**Airports:**
- Depart: PVG (Pudong) or SHA (Hongqiao)
- Arrive: HKG (Hong Kong International)

**Booking:** Google Flights / Skyscanner (6-8 weeks ahead for best prices)

### Train Options

**G99 High-Speed Train**
- Departs: 13:53 from Shanghai Hongqiao Station
- Arrives: 21:56 at Hong Kong West Kowloon Station
- Classes: 2nd class ($124-202), 1st class ($202), business ($397)
- Immigration: Handled inside West Kowloon station (very convenient)

**G899 Overnight Sleeper** (Money-saving choice!)
- Departs: 20:15 from Shanghai Hongqiao
- Arrives: 07:24 next day at Hong Kong West Kowloon
- Cost: $100-140 (soft sleeper, 4-berth compartment)
- **Net savings**: Saves one hotel night ($30-80) + transportation
- Luggage: Carry-on only, 20 kg limit

### Booking China Trains (12306 App)

**Setup (Do This Before Trip):**
1. Download **12306** app (official China Railway app)
2. Verify passport in app (required for foreign travelers)
3. Add passenger details

**Booking Process:**
- Tickets open **15 days before departure**
- For Aug 30 departure: **Aug 15, 12:00 AM China time** — SET ALARM!
- G99 and G899 sell out quickly — book immediately when they open

**Pro Tips:**
- West Kowloon station immigration is faster than airport
- No baggage weight limit on trains (be reasonable)
- Bring your passport — required for boarding

## Route 4: Kyoto → Tokyo

### Shinkansen Nozomi (Recommended)

- **Duration:** 2h 15m
- **Cost:** Y14,720 (~$95) one-way reserved seat
- **Frequency:** Every 10 minutes
- **Route:** Kyoto Station → Tokyo Station

### Booking via SmartEX App

**Setup:**
1. Download **SmartEX** app (official JR Tokai app)
2. Create account (English interface available)
3. Add credit card

**Booking Process:**
- Book **1-2 weeks before travel** (July 2026)
- Choose Nozomi for fastest (2h 15m) or Hikari if using JR Pass (2h 40m)

**Seat Selection:**
- **Left side (E seats)** facing Mt. Fuji views when traveling to Tokyo
- Window seats recommended for scenery

### JR Pass Analysis

**Is JR Pass worth it for this trip?**

| Option | Cost | Covered |
|--------|------|---------|
| JR Pass (7-day) | ~$198 | Hikari/Kodama only (not Nozomi) |
| Individual tickets | ~$95 | Any train |

**Verdict:** Not worth it for Osaka-Kyoto-Tokyo route alone. You'd save $226 with individual tickets.

### Alternative: Overnight Bus

- **Cost:** Y3,000-6,000 (~$20-50)
- **Saves:** One hotel night
- **Trade-off:** Less comfortable, arrives early morning

## Local Transit Cards & Payment

### Japan: Welcome Suica Mobile

- **Setup:** Add to Apple Wallet before trip (iPhone only)
- **Cost:** Free (no deposit)
- **Validity:** 180 days from issuance
- **Usage:** All JR, subway, private trains, buses, convenience stores

**Physical IC Card Alternative:**
- Suica (JR East) or ICOCA (JR West)
- Cost: Y2,000 (Y500 deposit + Y1,500 balance)
- All IC cards interchangeable nationwide since 2013

### Hong Kong: Octopus Card

- **Standard:** HK$150 (HK$50 refundable + HK$100 balance)
- **Tourist Version:** HK$39, no deposit
- **Usage:** MTR, buses, trams, Star Ferry, 7-Eleven, McDonald's

### China: Alipay & WeChat Pay

- **Alipay:** Link international credit card before trip
- **WeChat Pay:** Setup in WeChat app
- **Metro:** 3-15 RMB per trip ($0.40-2)
- **Didi:** China's Uber (accepts international cards)

## Money-Saving Transport Tips

1. **Skip the JR Pass** — Individual tickets cheaper for Osaka-Kyoto-Tokyo route
2. **Take overnight train** Shanghai-Hong Kong (G899) — saves hotel night
3. **Book flights 6-8 weeks ahead** — saves $100-300
4. **Use IC cards** in Japan — small discounts vs single tickets
5. **Nankai Rapi:t** from KIX — cheaper than JR Haruka
6. **Eat at convenience stores** in Japan — $3-5 meals vs $15-20 restaurants

## Transport Warnings

### Typhoon Season (August-September)

**Hong Kong:**
- Monitor Hong Kong Observatory
- **Signal 8 or above:** Stay indoors, all transport shuts down
- MTR, buses, ferries, flights all suspended

**Japan:**
- Trains may slow down or stop
- Flights may be delayed/cancelled
- Most Shinkansen lines have weather protection

**China:**
- Flights may be delayed
- High-speed trains usually operate normally

### Other Warnings

| Situation | Warning |
|-----------|---------|
| **China trains** | Strict — don't miss departure, no easy changes |
| **Shinkansen** | Can usually take next train same-day if you miss yours |
| **Budget airlines** | Baggage fees often extra, check before booking |
| **Passport required** | Always carry for train travel in China |
| **12306 app** | Must verify passport before booking |
