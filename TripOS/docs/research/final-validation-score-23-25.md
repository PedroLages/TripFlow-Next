### 5. Phase 5 Development: Blind Budgeting Feature

**Core requirements** (from expert validation):
1. **Anonymous budget submission**: Each member enters max budget privately
2. **Group max calculation**: Algorithm calculates maximum affordable for group
3. **Privacy by design**: RLS policies + encryption, no budget leaks
4. **Filter suggestions**: Show only activities group can afford
5. **Explain transparency**: "How it works" educational modal

**Technical architecture**:
- Supabase Row-Level Security (RLS) for budget privacy
- Edge functions for blind budgeting algorithm (server-side, not client)
- Real-time updates (group max changes as members join/leave)
- Audit trail (who submitted budgets, timestamps, no amounts visible)

**Success metrics**:
- 40% of trips use blind budgeting (validate unique value)
- 60% of members submit budgets (validate adoption)
- <5% support tickets about privacy (validate trust)
- NPS >40 (validate satisfaction)

## Risk Register

### Risk 1: Users Don't Trust Blind Budgeting
**Probability**: Medium
**Impact**: High
**Mitigation**:
- Phase 3 interviews to test trust/understanding
- Clear "How it works" explainers
- Opt-in approach (not forced)
- Transparent privacy policy

### Risk 2: Problem Real But Users Prefer Direct Conversations
**Probability**: Low
**Impact**: High
**Mitigation**:
- Phase 3 interviews to test preference
- Offer both modes: anonymous OR direct
- Don't force anonymity if groups prefer transparency

### Risk 3: Wanderlog Adds Blind Budgeting First
**Probability**: Low-Medium
**Impact**: Medium
**Mitigation**:
- Move fast (Phase 5 development = 4 weeks)
- Expert validation is our differentiator ("we built what experts recommend")
- First-mover advantage in UK market

### Risk 4: Phase 3 Interviews Show Weak Demand
**Probability**: Low
**Impact**: High
**Mitigation**:
- If 12+ of 15-20 say "I'd use this" → proceed
- If 8-11 say "I'd use this" → iterate on concept/UX
- If <8 say "I'd use this" → deprioritize, build other features first
